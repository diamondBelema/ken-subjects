    G6P accumulates and inhibits hexokinase to prevent unnecessary phosphorylation.

    active when blood glucose is high (postprandial).'

  front: 'Step 5 of glycolysis: Why is triose phosphate isomerase important?'

    the stoichiometry.

  front: Why is lactate formation obligatory under anaerobic conditions?
  back: Without O₂, NADH cannot donate electrons to ETC. Lactate dehydrogenase regenerates
    NAD⁺ from NADH, allowing glycolysis to continue. Without NAD⁺ regeneration, glycolysis
    stops and the cell dies.
