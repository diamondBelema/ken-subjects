# Pyruvate Decarboxylation & TCA Cycle

---

## Part 1: Pyruvate Decarboxylation

### Overview

- Under aerobic conditions, **pyruvate dehydrogenase complex (PDH)** catalyzes the decarboxylation of pyruvate to yield **Acetyl-CoA** and **CO₂**
- This is an **irreversible reaction** that links glycolysis and the citric acid cycle
- The conversion consists of three steps: **decarboxylation**, **oxidation**, and **transfer** of the acetyl group to CoA
- Reaction: Pyruvate + CoA + NAD⁺ → **Acetyl-CoA** + CO₂ + NADH

### Pyruvate Dehydrogenase Complex (PDH)

The complex has **3 enzymes**:

| Enzyme | Name | Role |
|--------|------|------|
| **E1** | Pyruvate dehydrogenase | Decarboxylates pyruvate; uses **TPP** |
| **E2** | Dihydrolipoyl transacetylase | Transfers acetyl group to CoA; uses **lipoamide** |
| **E3** | Dihydrolipoyl dehydrogenase | Regenerates oxidized lipoamide; uses **FAD** |

### 5 Cofactors Required

| Cofactor | Full Name | Role |
|----------|-----------|------|
| **TPP** | Thiamine pyrophosphate | Binds pyruvate on E1; decarboxylation |
| **Lipoamide** | Lipoic acid (on E2) | Acetyl group carrier; swings between E1 and E2 |
| **CoA** | Coenzyme A | Accepts acetyl group → Acetyl-CoA |
| **FAD** | Flavin adenine dinucleotide | Oxidizes reduced lipoamide (on E3) |
| **NAD⁺** | Nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide | Final electron acceptor → NADH |

### Mechanism (5 Steps)

1. **Pyruvate is decarboxylated** at the active site of E1 → substituted TPP intermediate + **CO₂** (first product)
2. **E2 inserts lipoyl-lysine arm** into E1 channel → E1 transfers acetyl group to lipoamide
3. **Acetyl group transferred to CoA** → **Acetyl-CoA** (second product) leaves. Reduced lipoyl arm swings to E3.
4. **Lipoamide reoxidized by FAD** at E3 active site
5. **FADH₂ reoxidized by NAD⁺** → **NADH** (final product). Reactivated lipoamide ready for next cycle.

> **Key Point**: E3 is **identical** in both PDH and α-ketoglutarate dehydrogenase complexes.

## Part 2: TCA Cycle (Citric Acid Cycle / Krebs Cycle)

### Overview

- **Location**: Mitochondrial matrix
- **Discovered by**: Hans Krebs (1937)
- **Also called**: Krebs cycle, Citric acid cycle, Tricarboxylic acid (TCA) cycle
- Called the "**final common pathway**" because most cellular energy from food molecules is harnessed here
- **Amphibolic pathway**: serves both catabolic and anabolic functions

### Why "Tricarboxylic Acid"?

- Most intermediates contain **three carboxylic acid groups**
- **Citric acid** (first intermediate) is a tricarboxylic acid

### Overview of the Cycle

1. **Acetyl-CoA (2C)** condenses with **oxaloacetate (4C)** → **citrate (6C)**
2. Citrate is isomerized → **isocitrate (6C)**
3. Isocitrate is oxidatively decarboxylated → **α-ketoglutarate (5C)** + CO₂
4. α-ketoglutarate is oxidatively decarboxylated → **succinyl-CoA (4C)** + CO₂
5. Succinyl-CoA → **succinate (4C)**
6. Succinate → **fumarate (4C)**
7. Fumarate → **malate (4C)**
8. Malate → **oxaloacetate (4C)** — cycle complete

> Two carbons enter as acetyl-CoA; two carbons leave as CO₂. Oxaloacetate is regenerated.

---

### Step 1: Citrate Synthase

| | |
|---|---|
| **Reaction** | Acetyl-CoA (2C) + Oxaloacetate (4C) → **Citrate (6C)** + CoA |
| **Mechanism** | OAA first condenses with acetyl-CoA → **citryl-CoA** (high-energy thioester intermediate) → hydrolyzed to citrate + CoA |
| **Key Point** | Hydrolysis of citryl-CoA drives the reaction forward (irreversible) |
| **Regulation** | Inhibited by **ATP**, citrate, NADH, succinyl-CoA |

---

### Step 2: Aconitase

| | |
|---|---|
| **Reaction** | Citrate → **Isocitrate** (via *cis*-aconitate intermediate) |
| **Mechanism** | Dehydration followed by rehydration (interchange of H and OH) |
| **Key Point** | Enzyme is **stereospecific** — catalyzes both elimination and addition of water |
| **Iron-sulfur enzyme** | Contains a [4Fe-4S] cluster |

---

### Step 3: Isocitrate Dehydrogenase ⭐ RATE-LIMITING STEP

| | |
|---|---|
| **Reaction** | Isocitrate → **α-Ketoglutarate** (5C) + CO₂ + NADH |
| **Mechanism** | 2-step reaction: isocitrate → **oxalosuccinate** (unstable β-ketoacid intermediate) → decarboxylation → α-ketoglutarate |
| **Key Points** | First **oxidative decarboxylation** in the cycle. C-6 → C-5 (first carbon of acetyl-CoA removed as CO₂). First NADH generated. |
| **Regulation** | ⭐ **Rate-limiting enzyme**. Inhibited by **ATP** and **NADH**. Stimulated by **ADP** and **NAD⁺**. |

---

### Step 4: α-Ketoglutarate Dehydrogenase Complex

| | |
|---|---|
| **Reaction** | α-Ketoglutarate + CoA + NAD⁺ → **Succinyl-CoA** (4C) + CO₂ + NADH |
| **Structure** | Homologous to PDH complex |
| **E1** | α-Ketoglutarate dehydrogenase (with **TPP**) |
| **E2** | Dihydrolipoyl succinyltransferase (with **lipoamide**) |
| **E3** | Dihydrolipoyl dehydrogenase (with **FAD**) — **identical to E3 of PDH** |
| **Key Points** | Decarboxylation of an α-ketoacid → high-energy thioester with CoA. Same coenzymes and mechanism as PDH. |
| **Regulation** | Inhibited by **succinyl-CoA** and **NADH** (product inhibition), and high energy charge |

---

### Step 5: Succinyl-CoA Synthetase

| | |
|---|---|
| **Reaction** | Succinyl-CoA + GDP + Pi → **Succinate** (4C) + GTP + CoA |
| **Key Points** | ΔG°' of succinyl-CoA hydrolysis ≈ -8 kcal/mol (comparable to ATP at -7.3 kcal/mol). Free energy conserved as **GTP** (or ATP in plants/some bacteria). |
| **Type** | **Substrate-level phosphorylation** — only step in TCA cycle that directly yields a high-phosphoryl-transfer-potential compound |
| **Mechanism** | Phosphoryl group transferred first to enzyme, then to nucleoside diphosphate |

> **Note**: In mammals, this produces GTP which can be converted to ATP by nucleoside diphosphate kinase.

---

### Step 6: Succinate Dehydrogenase Complex

| | |
|---|---|
| **Reaction** | Succinate → **Fumarate** (4C) + FADH₂ |
| **Structure** | Complex of several polypeptides, **FAD** prosthetic group, and **iron-sulfur clusters** |
| **Location** | **Embedded in the inner mitochondrial membrane** (only TCA enzyme in membrane; also Complex II of ETC) |
| **Key Points** | Electrons transferred: succinate → FAD → **ubiquinone (Q)** in ETC. Stereospecific — only **trans** isomer (fumarate) is formed. |

---

### Step 7: Fumarase

| | |
|---|---|
| **Reaction** | Fumarate + H₂O → **L-Malate** (4C) |
| **Mechanism** | Stereospecific **trans** addition of H₂O across the double bond |
| **Key Point** | Only the **L-isomer** of malate is formed |

---

### Step 8: Malate Dehydrogenase

| | |
|---|---|
| **Reaction** | L-Malate + NAD⁺ → **Oxaloacetate** (4C) + NADH + H⁺ |
| **Key Points** | Oxaloacetate regenerated → cycle complete. Thermodynamically unfavorable (ΔG°' very positive), but driven forward by removal of OAA (by citrate synthase) and low [OAA]. |
| **NADH produced** | Third NADH of the cycle |

---

## Energy Yield Per Turn of TCA Cycle

| Product | Quantity | ATP Equivalent (via ETC) |
|---------|----------|-------------------------|
| **NADH** | 3 | 3 × 2.5 = **7.5 ATP** |
| **FADH₂** | 1 | 1 × 1.5 = **1.5 ATP** |
| **GTP** (≡ ATP) | 1 | **1 ATP** |
| **Total** | | **10 ATP per acetyl-CoA** |

### Accounting Per Turn

- 2 carbon atoms enter (as acetyl-CoA)
- 2 carbon atoms leave (as 2 CO₂)
- 4 pairs of hydrogen atoms leave via 4 oxidation reactions:
  - 3 NAD⁺ → 3 NADH
  - 1 FAD → 1 FADH₂
- 1 GTP formed
- 2 H₂O consumed

> **Note**: Older estimates use 3 ATP per NADH and 2 ATP per FADH₂, giving ~12 ATP per turn. Modern estimates use 2.5 per NADH and 1.5 per FADH₂, giving ~10 ATP per turn.

## Regulation of TCA Cycle

### Site 1 — Citrate Synthase (Step 1)

| Effect | Molecules |
|--------|----------|
| **Inhibited by** | **ATP**, citrate, NADH, succinyl-CoA |

### Site 2 — Isocitrate Dehydrogenase (Step 3) ⭐

| Effect | Molecules |
|--------|----------|
| **Inhibited by** | **ATP**, **NADH** |
| **Stimulated by** | **ADP**, **NAD⁺** |
| **Note** | Rate-limiting enzyme of the cycle |

### Site 3 — α-Ketoglutarate Dehydrogenase (Step 4)

| Effect | Molecules |
|--------|----------|
| **Inhibited by** | **Succinyl-CoA**, **NADH**, high energy charge |
| **Note** | Similar regulation to PDH complex |

### General Principles

- TCA cycle regulated by **substrate availability** (acetyl-CoA, NAD⁺, ADP)
- Inhibited when energy charge is **high** (ATP, NADH accumulate)
- Stimulated when energy charge is **low** (ADP, NAD⁺ accumulate)

## Amphibolic Nature of TCA Cycle

The TCA cycle is **amphibolic** — it serves both:

### Catabolic Functions
- Oxidation of acetyl-CoA from carbohydrates, fats, and proteins
- Generation of NADH and FADH₂ for ATP production

### Anabolic Functions (Biosynthetic Precursors)

| Intermediate | Biosynthetic Pathway |
|-------------|---------------------|
| **Citrate** | Fatty acid synthesis (exported to cytosol) |
| **α-Ketoglutarate** | Amino acid synthesis (glutamate family), transamination |
| **Succinyl-CoA** | Heme synthesis (porphyrin ring) |
| **Oxaloacetate** | Gluconeogenesis (PEPCK), amino acid synthesis (aspartate family) |
| **Malate** | Gluconeogenesis (malate shuttle) |
| **Fumarate** | Urea cycle |

### Replenishing Reactions (Anaplerosis)

When intermediates are siphoned off for biosynthesis, they must be replenished:

| Reaction | Enzyme | Reaction |
|----------|--------|----------|
| **Pyruvate → Oxaloacetate** | Pyruvate carboxylase | Pyruvate + CO₂ + ATP → OAA + ADP + Pi |
| **PEP → Oxaloacetate** | PEP carboxykinase | PEP + CO₂ → OAA (in reverse direction) |
| **Aspartate → Oxaloacetate** | Transamination | Aspartate + α-ketoglutarate → OAA + glutamate |
| **Glutamate → α-Ketoglutarate** | Glutamate dehydrogenase | Glutamate → α-KG + NH₃ |

## TCA Cycle — Take-Home Summary

- Glycolysis oxidizes sugar to pyruvate → converted to Acetyl-CoA in mitochondria
- Proteins and fatty acids also broken down to yield Acetyl-CoA
- Acetyl units oxidized to CO₂ in mitochondrial matrix by TCA cycle
- Energy released captured by NAD⁺ → NADH and FAD → FADH₂
- These reducing equivalents transfer electrons to **ETC** for ATP synthesis (oxidative phosphorylation)
- **1 acetyl-CoA ≈ 10 ATP** (modern estimate)
- Two turns of TCA per glucose molecule → ~20 ATP from TCA per glucose
