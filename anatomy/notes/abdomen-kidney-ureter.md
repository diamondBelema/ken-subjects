# Kidney and Ureter

---

## Kidney

### General

- Paired, bean-shaped organs
- Location: **retroperitoneal**, on posterior abdominal wall
- Right kidney slightly lower than left (due to liver)
- Extends from approximately **T12 to L3** vertebral levels
- Length: ~11 cm; width: ~6 cm; thickness: ~3 cm
- Weight: ~150 g each

### External Features

- **Borders**: medial (concave, contains hilum) and lateral (convex)
- **Surfaces**:
  - **Anterior**: related to viscera (varies by side)
  - **Posterior**: related to posterior abdominal wall muscles
- **Poles**: superior and inferior

### Hilum

- Located on the medial concave border
- Structures passing through (from anterior to posterior):
  1. **Renal vein**
  2. **Renal artery**
  3. **Renal pelvis** (and ureter)
- Upper pole closer to midline; lower pole more lateral

### Relations

#### Right Kidney

| Surface | Relations |
|---|---|
| **Anterior** | Liver (bare area), right colic flexure, second part of duodenum, hepatic flexure |
| **Posterior** | Diaphragm, 12th rib, psoas major, quadratus lumborum, subcostal nerve, iliohypogastric nerve, ilioinguinal nerve |

#### Left Kidney

| Surface | Relations |
|---|---|
| **Anterior** | Stomach, spleen, splenic flexure, pancreas (body and tail), jejunum |
| **Posterior** | Same as right (diaphragm, 12th rib, psoas major, quadratus lumborum, nerves) |

### Peritoneal Relations

- Kidneys are **primarily retroperitoneal**
- Anterior surface covered by peritoneum (except where related to other retroperitoneal structures)
- Right kidney: peritoneum between kidney and liver (bare area posteriorly)

---

### Internal Structure

#### Coverings

1. **Renal capsule**: thin, tough fibrous layer directly on kidney surface
2. **Perirenal (adipose) capsule**: fat surrounding the capsule; provides protection
3. **Renal fascia (Gerota's fascia)**: outermost layer; encloses kidney and suprarenal gland; connects to surrounding structures

#### Cortex

- Outer region, **~1 cm thick**
- Granular appearance
- Contains:
  - **Renal corpuscles** (glomeruli + Bowman's capsules)
  - **Proximal and distal convoluted tubules**
  - **Cortical (arcuate) blood vessels**
  - Extends between medullary pyramids as **renal columns (of Bertin)**

#### Medulla

- Inner region, composed of **8-18 renal pyramids (medullary pyramids)**
- Striated appearance (due to parallel arrangement of tubules)
- Each pyramid:
  - **Base** faces the cortex
  - **Apex (renal papilla)** projects into a minor calyx
- Contains:
  - **Loops of Henle** (thick and thin segments)
  - **Collecting ducts**
  - **Vasa recta**

#### Renal Columns (of Bertin)

- Extensions of cortical tissue between medullary pyramids
- Contain blood vessels and nerves passing between cortex and medulla

#### Calyces

- **Minor calyces** (8-18): cup-shaped; surround renal papillae; collect urine from papillae
- **Major calyces** (2-3): formed by convergence of minor calyces
- **Renal pelvis**: funnel-shaped; formed by convergence of major calyces; continuous with ureter at the hilum

#### Renal Pelvis

- Funnel-shaped dilatation of the upper end of the ureter
- Lies within the renal sinus
- Divided into:
  - **Extrarenal part**: outside the hilum
  - **Intrarenal part**: within the sinus, divided into major calyces

### Nephron Structure

The nephron is the structural and functional unit of the kidney (~1 million per kidney).

#### Parts of the Nephron

| Part | Location | Function |
|---|---|---|
| **Renal corpuscle** (Bowman's capsule + glomerulus) | Cortex | Filtration |
| **Proximal convoluted tubule (PCT)** | Cortex | Reabsorption (~65% of filtrate); secretion |
| **Loop of Henle** | Cortex → medulla → cortex | Concentration of urine; countercurrent multiplication |
| **Distal convoluted tubule (DCT)** | Cortex | Fine-tuning; secretion; hormonal regulation |
| **Collecting duct** | Cortex → medulla | Final concentration of urine; ADH-dependent water reabsorption |

#### Types of Nephrons

| Type | Glomerulus Location | Loop of Henle | Function |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Cortical nephrons** (~85%) | Outer cortex | Short loops | Filtration and reabsorption |
| **Juxtamedullary nephrons** (~15%) | Near medulla | Long loops (deep into medulla) | Urine concentration |

### Blood Supply of the Kidney

The kidneys receive approximately **20-25% of cardiac output**.

#### Arterial Pathway

1. **Abdominal aorta** → **renal artery** (one per kidney; arise at L1-L2)
   - Right renal artery is longer; passes behind IVC
2. Renal artery enters hilum → divides into **segmental arteries** (5 segments: apical, superior, middle, inferior, posterior)
3. Segmental arteries → **interlobar arteries** (travel through renal columns)
4. Interlobar arteries → **arcuate arteries** (at corticomedullary junction)
5. Arcuate arteries → **interlobular (cortical radial) arteries** (radiate into cortex)
6. Interlobular arteries → **afferent arterioles** → **glomeruli** (capillary tufts)
7. Glomerulus drains via **efferent arterioles**

#### Post-Glomerular Circulation

| Pathway | Description |
|---|---|
| **Peritubular capillaries** | Surround PCT and DCT in cortex; reabsorption |
| **Vasa recta** | Long, straight capillaries descending with loops of Henle in medulla; countercurrent exchange |

#### Venous Drainage

Efferent arterioles → peritubular capillaries/vasa recta → **peritubular venules** → **interlobular veins** → **arcuate veins** → **interlobar veins** → **renal vein** → **IVC**

#### Portal System of the Kidney

- **Glomerular capillaries** → **efferent arterioles** → **peritubular capillaries**
- This is a **portal system** (two capillary beds connected by an arteriole)

## Ureter

### General

- Muscular tube **25-30 cm long**
- Connects **renal pelvis** to the **urinary bladder**
- Transports urine by **peristalsis**

### Course

1. Begins at the **ureteropelvic junction** (renal pelvis narrows into ureter)
2. Descends along the anterior surface of **psoas major muscle**
3. Crosses the **pelvic brim** (at the bifurcation of common iliac arteries)
4. Enters the pelvis and runs forward and medially along the lateral pelvic wall
5. Passes through the **ureteric tunnel** in the wall of the bladder
6. Opens into the bladder at the **ureteric orifice** (at the superolateral angles of the **trigone**)

### Constrictions (Clinical Significance for Kidney Stones)

| Location | Distance from Renal Pelvis |
|---|---|
| **Ureteropelvic junction** | 0 cm (start) |
| **Pelvic brim** (crossing iliac vessels) | ~10 cm |
| **Ureterovesical junction** (entry into bladder wall) | ~25 cm |

### Blood Supply

The ureter receives blood supply along its entire length from multiple sources:

| Region | Arterial Supply |
|---|---|
| **Upper third** | Renal artery and gonadal artery |
| **Middle third** | Abdominal aorta, gonadal artery, common iliac artery |
| **Lower third** | Internal iliac artery branches (superior vesical, uterine, vaginal, middle rectal) |

> Blood supply runs in the **adventitial** layer; the ureter can be mobilized safely as long as the adventitia is preserved in segments.

### Lymph Drainage

- **Upper part**: lumbar (lateral aortic) lymph nodes
- **Lower part**: internal iliac lymph nodes

### Nerve Supply

- Sympathetic: **T10-L1** (via lumbar splanchnic nerves)
- Parasympathetic: **S2-S4** (via pelvic splanchnic nerves)
- Sensory (pain): travels with sympathetic fibers

> Referred pain from ureteric colic: **T10-L1 dermatomes** (loin to groin: iliac fossa, groin, medial thigh, labia/scrotum)

### Histology

| Layer | Description |
|---|---|
| **Mucosa** | Transitional epithelium (urothelium) with lamina propria; folds when empty |
| **Muscularis** | Outer longitudinal and inner circular smooth muscle (upper 2/3); adds outer longitudinal layer in lower 1/3 |
| **Adventitia** | Connective tissue merging with surrounding structures |

### Clinical Notes

- **Ureteric (renal) colic**: excruciating pain caused by kidney stones lodged in the ureter; spasm of smooth muscle
- **Pelvic brim**: common site for stone impaction
- **Vesicoureteric junction**: narrowest point; stones may cause obstruction and hydronephrosis
- In females, ureter passes **1.5 cm lateral** to the cervix — at risk during hysterectomy
- In males, ureter passes **posterior to** the vas deferens
