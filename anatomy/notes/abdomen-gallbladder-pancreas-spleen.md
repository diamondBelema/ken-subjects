# Gallbladder, Pancreas, and Spleen

---

## Gallbladder

### Gross Anatomy

- Pear-shaped sac on the **inferior (visceral) surface of the liver** in the gallbladder fossa
- Capacity: **30-50 mL** (concentrates bile by absorbing water)
- Length: **7-10 cm**; width: 3-4 cm

### Parts

| Part | Description |
|---|---|
| **Fundus** | Rounded blind end; projects below inferior liver margin; contacts anterior abdominal wall at tip of 9th right costal cartilage (transpyloric plane) |
| **Body** | Largest portion; lies against visceral surface of liver; directed upward, backward, and to the left |
| **Infundibulum (Hartmann's pouch)** | Dilation between body and neck; common site for gallstone lodging |
| **Neck** | Narrow terminal portion; continues as cystic duct; contains **spiral folds (valves of Heister)** that maintain duct patency |

### Relations

- **Anteriorly**: anterior abdominal wall and inferior surface of liver
- **Posteriorly**: transverse colon and 1st and 2nd parts of duodenum

### Surface Anatomy

- Fundus at the point where the **lateral border of rectus abdominis** cuts the **costal margin** (tip of 9th right costal cartilage)

### Blood Supply

- **Arterial**: **cystic artery** (branch of right hepatic artery)
- **Venous**: cystic vein drains directly into **portal vein**
- Small arteries and veins also run between liver and gallbladder

### Nerve Supply

- **Sympathetic and parasympathetic** (vagal) fibers from the **celiac plexus**
- Gallbladder contracts in response to **cholecystokinin (CCK)**

### Lymph Drainage

- Cystic lymph node (near neck) → hepatic nodes → celiac nodes

### Biliary System

**Extrahepatic biliary tract:**

1. **Right and left hepatic ducts** emerge from porta hepatis and unite to form the **common hepatic duct**
2. Common hepatic duct (~4 cm) joined by **cystic duct** (~3.8 cm, S-shaped) to form the **common bile duct** (CBD)
3. **CBD** (~8 cm) courses through lesser omentum → behind 1st part of duodenum → groove on posterior surface of pancreatic head
4. Opens into 2nd part of duodenum at the **hepatopancreatic ampulla (ampulla of Vater)** via the **major duodenal papilla**
5. Regulated by the **sphincter of Oddi**

**Cystohepatic triangle (of Calot):**
- **Medially**: common hepatic duct
- **Laterally**: cystic duct
- **Superiorly**: cystic artery
- **Contents**: right hepatic artery, cystic artery, Lund's lymph node, connective tissue, lymphatics

### Clinical Notes

- **Gallstones (cholelithiasis)**: cholesterol or pigment concretions; more common in females
- **Cholecystitis**: inflammation from cystic duct obstruction; pain at 9th costal cartilage and lateral rectus border
- **Cholecystectomy**: surgical removal; must safeguard contents of cystohepatic triangle

---

### Histology of the Gallbladder

**Key distinguishing features**: NO muscularis mucosae and NO submucosa.

#### Layer 1: Mucosa

- **Epithelium**: simple columnar absorptive cells (**cholangiocytes**) with microvilli
  - No goblet cells normally
- **Lamina propria**: loose vascular connective tissue with capillaries, lymphocytes, plasma cells, macrophages
- Mucosa forms **numerous folds** when empty (disappear when distended)
- Folds resemble villi but lack central lacteal and are not associated with crypts
- **Mucus-secreting cells** only in the **neck region**

#### Layer 2: Muscularis

- **Irregularly arranged smooth muscle bundles** intermixed with collagen and elastic fibers
- No distinct circular or longitudinal layers (unlike intestine)
- Contracts in response to **CCK** to expel bile

#### Layer 3: Perimuscular Connective Tissue

- Dense connective tissue with larger blood vessels, lymphatics, nerves, adipose tissue

#### Layer 4: Serosa or Adventitia

- **Free peritoneal surface** = **serosa** (mesothelium + CT)
- **Hepatic surface** = **adventitia** (CT only, anchors to liver)

#### Rokitansky-Aschoff Sinuses

- Epithelial outpouchings that extend into the **muscular layer**
- Normal anatomical variant; become more prominent in **chronic cholecystitis**
- May trap bile, debris, or bacteria → persistent inflammation

#### Gallbladder vs Small Intestine

| Feature | Gallbladder | Small Intestine |
|---|---|---|
| Goblet cells | Absent | Present |
| Villi | Absent | Present |
| Crypts of Lieberkühn | Absent | Present |
| Muscularis mucosae | Absent | Present |
| Submucosa | Absent | Present |
| Main function | Concentrate bile | Absorb nutrients |

## Pancreas

### Gross Anatomy

- **Mixed gland**: 98% exocrine, 2% endocrine
- **Retroperitoneal organ**
- C-shaped relationship with the duodenum
- Surface anatomy: neck lies on the **transpyloric plane (L1)**

### Parts

| Part | Key Features |
|---|---|
| **Head** | Broadest part; moulded to C-shaped duodenum; lies over IVC and renal veins (level L2); terminal bile duct indents or tunnels posteriorly; **uncinate process** hooks behind SMA |
| **Neck** | Constricted portion; lies in front of **beginning of portal vein** |
| **Body** | Triangular in cross section with 3 surfaces (anterosuperior, anteroinferior, posterior) and 3 borders (superior, anterior, inferior); **tuber omental** where anterior surface joins neck |
| **Tail** | Passes in **splenorenal ligament** to hilum of spleen |

### Relations

- **Anterior**: transverse colon, transverse mesocolon, lesser sac, stomach
- **Posterior**: bile duct, portal vein, splenic vein, IVC, aorta, origin of SMA, left psoas, left suprarenal gland, left kidney, hilum of spleen

### Pancreatic Ducts

- **Main pancreatic duct (of Wirsung)**: begins in tail; runs full length; opens into 2nd part of duodenum at **major duodenal papilla** with bile duct
- **Accessory duct (of Santorini)**: when present, drains upper head; opens at **minor duodenal papilla**

### Sphincters

- Sphincter of the pancreatic duct
- Sphincter of the bile duct
- **Hepatopancreatic sphincter (sphincter of Oddi)**

### Blood Supply

- **Arterial**: splenic artery, superior and inferior pancreaticoduodenal arteries
- **Venous**: corresponding veins drain into the **portal system**

### Nerve Supply

- Sympathetic and parasympathetic (vagus nerve)

### Lymph Drainage

- Nodes along the supplying arteries → celiac and superior mesenteric nodes

---

### Histology of the Pancreas

#### Exocrine Pancreas (98%)

- **Compound tubuloacinar gland**
- Basic unit: **serous acinus** — pyramidal cells around a small lumen
  - **Basophilic base** (abundant RER for protein synthesis)
  - **Eosinophilic apex** (zymogen granules with inactive enzymes)
- **Centroacinar cells**: pale-staining; represent initial part of ductal system; add bicarbonate and water

**Ductal system:**
Centroacinar cells → intercalated ducts → intralobular ducts → interlobular ducts → **main pancreatic duct (Wirsung)**

**Enzymes secreted (inactive zymogens):**

| Enzyme | Substrate |
|---|---|
| **Amylase** | Carbohydrates |
| **Lipase** | Fats |
| **Trypsinogen** | Activated to trypsin by **enterokinase** (proteins) |
| **Chymotrypsinogen** | Activated to chymotrypsin (proteins) |

#### Endocrine Pancreas (2%)

- **Islets of Langerhans**: pale-staining, lightly vascularized clusters scattered among acini

| Cell Type | Hormone | Function |
|---|---|---|
| **Alpha (α) cells** | **Glucagon** | Raises blood glucose |
| **Beta (β) cells** | **Insulin** | Lowers blood glucose |
| **Delta (δ) cells** | **Somatostatin** | Inhibits alpha and beta cells |
| **PP cells** | **Pancreatic polypeptide** | Regulates pancreatic secretion |

- Endocrine secretions enter capillary network → glucose homeostasis

### Clinical Notes

- **Pancreatitis**: gallstones or alcohol most common causes; severe epigastric pain
- **Carcinoma of head**: obstructs bile duct → obstructive jaundice
- **Pseudocyst**: fluid collection in lesser sac from perforated gastric ulcer or pancreatitis

## Spleen

### Gross Anatomy

- **Largest single mass of lymphoid tissue**
- Location: **left hypochondrium**, deep to ribs **9, 10, and 11**
- Long axis along the shaft of the **10th rib**
- Separated from ribs by **diaphragm** and **costodiaphragmatic pleural recess**
- Ovoid shape with **notched anterior border**
- Normal spleen cannot be palpated

### Surfaces

| Surface | Relations |
|---|---|
| **Diaphragmatic** | Convex; fits diaphragm and adjacent ribs |
| **Visceral** | Related to viscera (stomach, tail of pancreas, left colic flexure, left kidney) |

### Borders

- **Anterior and superior**: sharp (anterior border notched)
- **Posterior (medial) and inferior**: rounded

### Peritoneal Reflections/Ligaments

- **Gastrosplenic ligament** → to greater curvature of stomach; carries **short gastric vessels** and **left gastroepiploic vessels**
- **Lienorenal (splenorenal) ligament** → to front of left kidney; carries **splenic vessels** and **tail of pancreas**

### Full Relations

- **Anteriorly (visceral surface)**: stomach, tail of pancreas, left colic flexure, left kidney
- **Posteriorly**: diaphragm (separating from left pleura, left lung, and ribs 9-11)
- **Inferiorly**: left colic flexure
- **Medially**: left kidney

### Blood Supply

- **Arterial**: **splenic artery** (largest branch of celiac trunk); tortuous course along upper border of pancreas; passes through lienorenal ligament; divides into 4-5 branches at the hilum
- **Venous**: **splenic vein** leaves hilum → runs behind tail and body of pancreas → joins **superior mesenteric vein** behind neck of pancreas to form **portal vein**
  - Tributaries: short gastric vein, left gastroepiploic vein, pancreatic veins, inferior mesenteric vein

### Nerve Supply

- Derived from **celiac plexus**; distributed mainly along splenic artery branches; vasomotor in function

### Lymph Drainage

- Hilar nodes → along course of splenic artery → **celiac lymph nodes**

---

### Histology of the Spleen

#### Red Pulp

- Composed of **splenic cords (cords of Billroth)** and **venous sinuses**
- Functions:
  - Filters blood
  - Removes old/damaged **RBCs** (macrophages)
  - Recycles iron from hemoglobin
  - Contains macrophages for phagocytosis

#### White Pulp

- Lymphoid tissue organized around **central arteries**
- Consists of:
  - **Periarteriolar lymphoid sheaths (PALS)**: T lymphocytes around central artery
  - **Lymphoid follicles (nodules)**: B lymphocytes; may contain germinal centers
  - **Marginal zone**: surrounds white pulp; contains macrophages and dendritic cells

#### Marginal Zone

- Interface between red and white pulp
- Contains specialized macrophages and dendritic cells
- Site of initial immune response to blood-borne antigens

#### Blood Flow Through Spleen

1. Blood enters via **splenic artery** → branches → **central arteries** (in white pulp)
2. Some blood empties directly into **red pulp** (open circulation)
3. Blood percolates through **splenic cords** → filtered by macrophages
4. Cleaned blood enters **venous sinuses** → collects into **splenic veins** → exits via hilum

#### Functions of the Spleen

- **Filtration** of blood (removal of old RBCs, pathogens)
- **Immune response** (white pulp: T and B cells)
- **Storage** of platelets and RBCs
- **Hematopoiesis** in fetus

### Clinical Notes

- **Rupture of spleen**: most frequently injured abdominal organ; blunt trauma → intraperitoneal hemorrhage and shock
- **Splenectomy**: total or subtotal; after total splenectomy, functions assumed by liver and bone marrow; greater susceptibility to certain bacterial infections
- **Splenomegaly**: enlargement due to disease (leukemia, hemolytic anemias, hypertension)
- **Accessory spleen**: common; ~1 cm; near hilum or in tail of pancreas
