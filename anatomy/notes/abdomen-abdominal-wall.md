# Anterolateral Abdominal Wall

## Boundaries of the Abdomen

- **Anterolaterally**: muscles forming the anterolateral abdominal wall
- **Posteriorly**: lumbar vertebrae and paravertebral muscles of the back
- **Superiorly**: diaphragm
- **Inferiorly**: pelvic inlet / pelvic diaphragm

---

## Skin and Superficial Fascia

- Skin is **thin anteriorly**, **thick posteriorly**
- **Tension lines run horizontally**
- Superficial fascia has two layers:
  - **Camper's fascia** — superficial fatty layer
  - **Scarpa's fascia** — deep membranous layer
- Scarpa's fascia extends into the perineum as **Colles fascia**
- In males: invests penis as **superficial penile fascia** and scrotum as **dartos fascia**
- In females: both layers continue into **labia majora**
- In the thigh, both layers blend with the **fascia lata**

### Applied Anatomy
- Extravasation of fluid
- Suture retention

---

## Blood Supply

- **Intercostal, subcostal, and lumbar arteries** — supply the flanks
- **Internal thoracic, superior and inferior epigastric arteries** — supply the ventral midline

### Venous Drainage

| Region | Drainage Pathway |
|---|---|
| Below umbilicus | Great saphenous vein via **superficial epigastric vein** |
| Above umbilicus | Axillary vein via **lateral thoracic vein** |
| Both connected by | **Thoracoepigastric vein** |

## Lymphatic Drainage

- **Above umbilicus** → anterior group of **axillary lymph nodes**
- **Below umbilicus** → medial group of **superficial inguinal nodes**

## Nerve Supply

- **Lower six intercostal nerves** and **subcostal nerve**

### Dermatomes

| Level | Region |
|---|---|
| **T7** | Epigastric region |
| **T10** | Umbilicus |
| **L1** | Suprapubic / hypogastric region |

## Regions of the Abdomen

Formed by **four imaginary lines** (two vertical, two horizontal):

- **Vertical lines**: right and left midclavicular lines (midpoint of clavicle to midinguinal point)
- **Horizontal lines**:
  - **Transpyloric line** — midway between jugular notch and pubic symphysis
  - **Intertubercular line** — across the tubercles of the iliac crest

### Nine Regions

| Right hypochondriac | Epigastric | Left hypochondriac |
|---|---|---|
| **Right lumbar** | **Umbilical** | **Left lumbar** |
| **Right iliac** | **Hypogastric** | **Left iliac** |

### Transpyloric Plane (L2)

- Cuts through the **pylorus of stomach**
- Passes through the **tip of 9th costal cartilages**
- **Conus medullaris** lies at this level
- **Horizontal part of duodenum** is found here
- **Neck of pancreas**, **fundus of gallbladder** located here
- **Portal vein** is formed at this level
- **Superior mesenteric artery** originates at this level

## Muscles of the Anterolateral Wall

### External Oblique

- **Largest and most superficial** muscle
- Anterior half is tendinous membrane (**external oblique aponeurosis**)

| Feature | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Origin** | External surfaces of last 8 ribs |
| **Insertion** | Linea alba, pubic tubercle, iliac crest |
| **Nerve supply** | Inferior six intercostal nerves and subcostal nerve |
| **Action** | Compression of abdomen and support of contents |

**Key notes:**
- Free lower aponeurotic border → **inguinal ligament (of Poupart)** attached to ASIS and pubic tubercle
- Forms **anterior border of lumbar triangle** (completed by internal oblique inferiorly and latissimus dorsi posteriorly)
- Above the midpoint of inguinal ligament is the **deep inguinal ring**
- Contributes to the **anterior layer of rectus sheath**

### Internal Oblique

- Lies immediately deep to external oblique
- Fibres run at **right angles** to external oblique

| Feature | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Origin** | Thoracolumbar fascia, anterior 2/3 of iliac crest, lateral half of inguinal ligament |
| **Insertion** | Inferior borders of 10-12 ribs, linea alba, pecten pubis |
| **Nerve supply** | Lower 6 intercostal nerves and first lumbar nerve |
| **Action** | Compresses the abdomen |

**Key notes:**
- Aponeurosis splits into **two layers** at the lateral edge of rectus abdominis to form the rectus sheath
- Aponeuroses of internal oblique and transversus abdominis join to form **conjoint tendon** below the umbilicus

### Transversus Abdominis

- **Innermost flat muscle**
- Contributes to the **posterior layer of rectus sheath**

| Feature | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Origin** | Lateral surfaces of 7-12 costal cartilages, thoracolumbar fascia, iliac crest, lateral 1/3 of inguinal ligament |
| **Insertion** | Linea alba, pubic crest, pecten pubis |
| **Nerve supply** | Lower 6 intercostal nerves, first lumbar nerve |
| **Action** | Compresses abdomen |

### Rectus Abdominis

- **Strap-like muscle** that broadens as it ascends into the rib cage
- Enclosed by **rectus sheath**

| Feature | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Origin** | Two heads: medial (anterior pubic symphysis) and lateral (upper pubic crest) |
| **Insertion** | Anterior surfaces of 5-7 costal cartilages and xiphoid process |
| **Nerve supply** | Lower 6 intercostal nerves |
| **Action** | Flexes trunk and compresses abdomen |

**Key notes:**
- Two recti separated by **linea alba**
- **Three tendinous intersections** on anterior surface at:
  - Umbilicus
  - Xiphisternum
  - Midpoint between umbilicus and xiphisternum

### Pyramidalis

- Small triangular muscle, **absent in 20%** of the population
- **Origin**: pubis and pubic symphysis
- **Insertion**: linea alba
- **Nerve supply**: lower 6 intercostal nerves
- **Action**: tenses the linea alba

### Linea Alba

- Strong fibrous midline structure
- Attached superiorly to **xiphoid process**, inferiorly to **pubic symphysis**
- Formed by aponeuroses of **external oblique, internal oblique, and transversus abdominis**
- Separates the two recti muscles

---

## Rectus Sheath

Strong fibrous tissue enclosing rectus abdominis and pyramidalis (when present).

### Formation in Three Regions

#### Above the Costal Margin
- Posterior wall is **absent**
- Sheath formed by **external oblique aponeurosis** only
- Rectus abdominis rests directly on **costal cartilages and intercostal muscle**

#### Between Costal Margin and Arcuate Line
- **Internal oblique** aponeurosis splits into anterior and posterior layers at lateral border of rectus abdominis
- **Anterior layer** = external oblique + anterior layer of internal oblique
- **Posterior layer** = transversus abdominis + posterior layer of internal oblique

#### Below the Arcuate Line (halfway between umbilicus and pubic symphysis)
- Internal oblique aponeurosis passes **un-split in front** of rectus abdominis
- Transversus abdominis aponeurosis also passes anteriorly
- **Anterior wall** = all three aponeuroses (external oblique, internal oblique, transversus abdominis)
- **Posterior wall absent** — rectus lies directly on **transversalis fascia**

### Contents of Rectus Sheath

- Rectus abdominis
- Pyramidalis
- **Lower six intercostal nerves**
- **Superior epigastric artery**
- **Inferior epigastric artery**

### Applied Anatomy
- Rectus sheath hematoma
- Abdominal hernia

## Inguinal Canal

- **Oblique passage** through the anterior abdominal wall connecting extraperitoneal space to the scrotum (male) or labia majora (female)
- About **4 cm long**
- Lies above the **medial half of the inguinal ligament**
- Extends from **deep inguinal ring** to **superficial inguinal ring**

### Walls

| Wall | Structure |
|---|---|
| **Anterior** | Aponeuroses of external and internal oblique muscles |
| **Posterior** | Transversalis fascia; medially by **conjoint tendon** |
| **Roof (Superior)** | Internal oblique and transversus abdominis aponeuroses |
| **Floor (Inferior)** | Inguinal ligament; medially by **lacunar ligament** |

### Deep Inguinal Ring

- Oval opening in the **transversalis fascia**
- Lies about **1.25 cm above the midpoint** of inguinal ligament
- Transversalis fascia continues as **internal spermatic fascia** surrounding the spermatic cord
- **Inferior epigastric artery** crosses the posterior wall of the deep ring (gives **cremasteric branch**)

### Superficial Inguinal Ring

- **Triangular opening** in the **external oblique aponeurosis**
- Aponeurosis surrounds spermatic cord as **external spermatic fascia**
- Two crura:
  - **Lateral crus** → attached to pubic tubercle
  - **Medial crus** → attached to pubic symphysis

### Inguinal Triangle (of Hesselbach)

- **Lateral**: inferior epigastric artery
- **Medial**: lateral edge of rectus abdominis
- **Inferior**: inguinal ligament
- Hernia passing **lateral** to inferior epigastric artery (through deep ring) = **indirect**
- Hernia passing **medial** to inferior epigastric artery (through triangle) = **direct**

### Contents

**Male:**
- Spermatic cord
- Ilioinguinal nerve

**Female:**
- Round ligament of the uterus
- Ilioinguinal nerve
- Obliterated processus vaginalis

> Note: Ilioinguinal nerve does **not** enter through the deep ring; it **pierces the internal oblique** muscle.

### Constituents of the Spermatic Cord

- Vas deferens
- Artery to vas deferens
- Testicular artery
- Cremasteric artery
- **Pampiniform plexus** of veins
- Lymphatic vessels
- **Genital branch of genitofemoral nerve**
- Processus vaginalis

### Integrity of the Inguinal Canal

- Strength of **anterior wall muscles** and **medial posterior wall**
- **Conjoint tendon** strengthens posterior aspect of superficial ring
- Aponeuroses of three muscles and increased intra-abdominal pressure help close the canal between the rings
- Posterolaterally, transversalis fascia strengthened by **interfoveolar ligament** (tendinous fibres from transversus abdominis)

---

## Inguinal Hernias

| Feature | Indirect | Direct |
|---|---|---|
| **Route** | Through deep inguinal ring | Through inguinal triangle (Hesselbach) |
| **Relation to epigastric a.** | Lateral | Medial |
| **Most common in** | Males (any age) | Males >40 years |
| **Path** | Follows spermatic cord | Directly through posterior wall |
| **Often enters** | Scrotum | Does not typically enter scrotum |
| **Cause** | Persistent processus vaginalis | Weakness of posterior wall |
