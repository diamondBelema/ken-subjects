# Male Reproductive System

The male reproductive system consists of the **testes**, **epididymis**, **ductus (vas) deferens**, **urethra**, **penis**, and **accessory glands** (seminal vesicles, prostate, bulbourethral glands).

---

## Testes

### Location and General Features
- Paired, oval-shaped glands located in the **scrotum**
- Each testis produces **sperm** (in seminiferous tubules) and **testosterone** (from Leydig/interstitial cells)
- Testes develop retroperitoneally in the dorsal abdominal wall and migrate during fetal development into the scrotum via the **inguinal canal**

### Coverings (from superficial to deep)

| Covering | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **Skin of scrotum** | Thin, rugose, hairless; contains dartos muscle |
| **Dartos fascia/muscle** | Loose CT with smooth muscle; continuous with Scarpa's fascia; responsible for scrotal wrinkling |
| **External spermatic fascia** | From external oblique aponeurosis |
| **Cremaster muscle and fascia** | From internal oblique; elevates testis in response to cold/stress |
| **Internal spermatic fascia** | From transversalis fascia |
| **Tunica vaginalis** | Serous sac derived from peritoneum; parietal layer (attached to scrotum) and visceral layer (covers tunica albuginea anteriorly and laterally); contains vaginal cavity |
| **Tunica albuginea** | Dense fibrous capsule enclosing the testis |
| **Tunica vasculosa** | Vascular layer of loose CT deep to tunica albuginea |

### Internal Structure
- **Tunica albuginea** thickens posteriorly and extends inward to form the **mediastinum testis**
- **Septa** radiate from mediastinum, dividing the testis into **testicular lobules** (~250)
- Each lobule contains **2-3 seminiferous tubules**
- Seminiferous tubules are lined by **seminiferous epithelium** (several cell layers thick) on a basement membrane surrounded by **tunica propria**

### Seminiferous Tubules
- Site of **spermatogenesis**
- Contain **Sertoli cells** (sustentacular/support cells) and **germ cells** at various stages
- **Leydig cells** (interstitial cells): located between tubules; produce **testosterone**
- **Blood-testis barrier**: occluding junctions between Sertoli cells partition the lumen into:
  - **Basal compartment**: contains spermatogonia
  - **Adluminal compartment**: contains developing spermatocytes and spermatids
  - Protects developing germ cells from blood-borne toxins

### Blood Supply
- **Testicular artery**: branch of the abdominal aorta (runs retroperitoneally)
- **Pampiniform plexus of veins**: surrounds the testicular artery; thermoregulatory function; drains into the **testicular vein** (left drains into left renal vein; right drains directly into IVC)

### Lymphatic Drainage
- **Para-aortic lymph nodes** (following the testicular arteries)
- Important for metastasis of testicular cancer

### Nerve Supply
- Sympathetic fibres from T10-T11 (via testicular plexus)
- Follow the testicular vessels

## Epididymis

### Parts (from superior to inferior)
1. **Head (caput)**: superior pole of the testis; receives efferent ductules from the testis
2. **Body (corpus)**: posterolateral surface of the testis
3. **Tail (cauda)**: inferior pole; continues as the vas deferens

### Relations
- Lies along the **posterolateral** border of the testis
- Head is covered by the visceral layer of tunica vaginalis

### Function
- **Storage and maturation of sperm**
- Spermatozoa spend ~12 days traversing the epididymis, gaining motility
- The **duct of the epididymis** is ~6 m long (highly coiled)

### Blood Supply
- **Epididymal arteries**: branches from testicular artery and artery to vas deferens

## Vas Deferens (Ductus Deferens)

### Course and Parts
From the tail of the epididymis, the vas deferens ascends along the posterior border of the testis, then:

1. **Spermatic cord portion**: ascends through the inguinal canal within the spermatic cord
2. **Inguinal portion**: passes through the deep and superficial inguinal rings
3. **Inguinal (abdominal) portion**: enters the abdominal cavity via the deep inguinal ring
4. **Pelvic portion**: courses over the pelvic brim, along the lateral wall of the pelvis
5. **Ampulla**: the terminal portion dilates into the **ampulla**, behind the bladder

### Relations
- In the spermatic cord: surrounded by the three fascial layers (internal spermatic fascia, cremasteric fascia, external spermatic fascia)
- Crosses over the **ureter** at the pelvic brim ("water under the bridge")
- The ampulla lies medial to the seminal vesicle

### Structure
- Thick muscular wall (3 layers of smooth muscle: inner longitudinal, middle circular, outer longitudinal) — gives it a firm, cord-like feel
- Lined by pseudostratified columnar epithelium with stereocilia

### Ampulla
- Terminal portion enlarged into an ampulla
- Lumen larger than the vas deferens
- Mucosa has irregular branching folds and deep glandular crypts
- Lined by simple columnar or cuboidal epithelium
- Three layers of smooth muscle

### Blood Supply
- **Artery to the vas deferens**: branch of the inferior vesical artery (or umbilical artery)

### Lymphatic Drainage
- External and common iliac lymph nodes

## Seminal Vesicles

### General Features
- Pair of coiled, tubular glands in the male pelvis
- Each gland is about **3-5 cm long** (10-15 cm if uncoiled)
- Secretes **alkaline fluid** that makes up roughly **60-80% of semen** (rich in fructose)

### Location and Relations
- Lie **supero-posteriorly** to the prostate and **lateral** to the ampullae of the vas deferens
- **Anteriorly**: posterior wall of the bladder
- **Posteriorly**: separated from rectum by **rectoprostatic fascia (Denonvilliers' fascia)**
- **Inferiorly**: prostate
- **Medially**: ampulla of ductus deferens
- Separated from rectum by the **rectovesical pouch**

### Structure
- Single blind-ending, highly convoluted tube with many outpouchings → honeycomb-like inner appearance
- **Three tissue layers**:
  1. Mucosa: highly folded; pseudostratified columnar epithelium
  2. Muscularis: inner circular and outer longitudinal smooth muscle
  3. Adventitia: outer connective tissue layer

### Duct System
- Excretory duct at the base joins the **vas deferens** to form the **ejaculatory duct**
- Ejaculatory duct traverses the prostate and opens into the **prostatic urethra** (at the colliculus seminalis)

### Function
- Produces nutrient- and fructose-rich fluid (provides energy for sperm)
- Contributes to semen volume (up to 60-80%)
- **Sympathetic stimulation** contracts the muscular wall during ejaculation
- Forms a functional unit with the ampulla of the vas deferens and ejaculatory ducts

### Blood Supply
- **Inferior vesical artery** and **middle rectal artery** (branches of internal iliac artery)

### Nerve Supply
- **Parasympathetic**: from pelvic splanchnic nerves (S2-S4) via inferior hypogastric plexus → stimulates fluid secretion
- **Sympathetic**: from T12-L2 via superior hypogastric plexus → governs smooth muscle contraction during ejaculation

### Lymphatic Drainage
- Internal iliac lymph nodes

### Clinical
- **Seminal vesiculitis**: inflammation; pain in lower abdomen/perineum, painful ejaculation, haemospermia
- **Seminal vesicle cysts**: congenital or acquired; can cause pelvic pain and painful ejaculation

## Prostate Gland

### General Features
- Largest **accessory gland** in the male reproductive system
- Walnut-sized, **fibromuscular gland**
- Located below the bladder, in front of the rectum
- Wraps around the **proximal urethra**
- Contributes nutrient-rich fluids to semen

### Landmarks and Relations

| Surface | Relation |
|---------|----------|
| **Base** (superior) | Neck of urinary bladder; perforated by urethra |
| **Apex** (inferior) | Rests on external urethral sphincter and urogenital diaphragm; prostatic urethra emerges from front |
| **Posterior** | Separated from rectum by **rectovesical fascia (Denonvilliers' fascia)**; ejaculatory ducts pierce this surface |
| **Anterior** | Separated from pubic symphysis by retropubic fat and prostatic venous plexus; connected by puboprostatic ligament |
| **Inferolateral** | Levator ani and prostatic plexus |
| **Neurovascular bundles** | Run posterolaterally; control erectile function; at risk during radical prostatectomy |

### Lobes (Gross Anatomy — 5 lobes)

1. **Anterior lobe**: in front of urethra; small isthmus; mainly fibromuscular; little/no glandular tissue
2. **Posterior lobe**: behind urethra and ejaculatory ducts; below and behind median lobe; **most common site for carcinoma**
3. **Median lobe**: behind upper urethra, in front of ejaculatory ducts; contains glandular tissue; common site for **BPH**; produces elevation in trigone of bladder (**uvula vesicae**)
4. **Right and left lateral lobes**: on each side of urethra; contain many glands; form the main bulk

### Zones (Histology — McNeal's Classification)

| Zone | % of Gland | Location | Clinical Significance |
|------|-----------|----------|----------------------|
| **Peripheral zone (PZ)** | ~65-70% | Posterior and lateral aspects; palpable on DRE | **70-80% of prostate cancers** arise here |
| **Central zone (CZ)** | ~25% | Surrounds ejaculatory ducts | Relatively immune to hyperplasia; "Roman bridge" architecture |
| **Transitional zone (TZ)** | 5-10% | Directly surrounds prostatic urethra | **Primary site of BPH** |
| **Anterior fibromuscular stroma** | — | Anterior surface; non-glandular | Smooth muscle and fibrous tissue |

### Capsule
- **True capsule**: thin, strong connective tissue at periphery
- **False capsule**: condensation of pelvic fascia
- **Prostatic venous plexus** lies between true and false capsules

### Blood Supply
- **Prostatic arteries** from **inferior vesical artery** (branch of anterior division of internal iliac artery)
- May also branch from internal pudendal, obturator, or superior vesical arteries

### Venous Drainage
- **Prostatic venous plexus** (between true and false capsules)
- Joins vesicoprostatic plexus
- Receives deep dorsal vein of penis
- Drains into **internal iliac veins**
- Communicates with vertebral venous plexus → explains metastasis of prostate cancer to **sacrum and vertebrae**

### Nerve Supply
- **Parasympathetic** (S2-S4): pelvic splanchnic nerves → stimulates prostatic fluid secretion
- **Sympathetic** (T10-L2): superior hypogastric plexus → contracts prostatic smooth muscle and internal urethral sphincter during ejaculation

### Lymphatic Drainage
- Internal iliac, external iliac, and sacral lymph nodes
- Also drains to aortic nodes

### Clinical Correlations

| Condition | Key Features |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Benign Prostatic Hyperplasia (BPH)** | Non-cancerous enlargement; occurs in **transitional zone**; causes LUTS (difficulty starting, weak stream, nocturia); smooth, symmetrical, rubbery on DRE |
| **Prostatic Carcinoma** | Malignant; arises in **peripheral zone** (70-80%); may be hard, asymmetrical, nodular on DRE; can metastasise to lymph nodes and bones |
| **Prostatectomy** | Surgical removal of all or part of the prostate; for localised cancer or severe BPH |

**Distinguishing BPH from Cancer:**

| Feature | BPH | Prostate Cancer |
|---------|-----|-----------------|
| Location | Transitional zone (inner) | Peripheral zone (outer) |
| DRE feel | Smooth, symmetrical, rubbery | Hard, asymmetrical, nodular |
| Progression | Cannot spread; no cancer risk | Can metastasise |
| PSA | Elevated (more tissue) | Elevated (leaking cancer cells) |
| Cellular change | Hyperplasia (normal cells) | Neoplasia (abnormal cells) |

### Prostatic Urethra
- 2.5-3 cm long
- Features: **urethral crest** (midline ridge), **colliculus seminalis/verumontanum** (rounded eminence), **prostatic utricle** (small blind pouch from paramesonephric ducts), **prostatic sinus** (shallow depression on each side of crest)

## Penis (Phallus)

### Description
- Male organ of copulation; traversed by the urethra
- Also known as the **penis**
- Capable of erection due to engorgement with blood
- Functions: **sexual intercourse** and **micturition**

### Parts

#### Root (Radix)
- Situated in the **superficial perineal pouch**
- Attached to the inferior aspect of the urogenital diaphragm
- Comprises 3 masses of erectile tissue:
  - **Two crura** (right and left): continuation of corpora cavernosa
  - **Bulb of the penis**: continuation of corpus spongiosum

#### Body (Corpus)
- Free pendulous portion, anterior to scrotum
- Subdivided into: body proper, neck, and glans penis

### Erectile Tissue Structure

| Tissue | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Corpus cavernosum** (pair) | Form the greater part of the body; forward continuation of crura; surrounded by **tunica albuginea**; contains cavernous spaces |
| **Corpus spongiosum** | Forward continuation of the bulb; cylindrical; tapers then expands distally into the **glans penis**; surrounds the spongy urethra |

### Coverings (Superficial to Deep)
1. **Skin**: thin, delicate, dark, hairless; loosely attached; freely mobile
2. **Superficial fascia**: two layers:
   - Superficial (fat-free, loose areolar tissue; may contain peripenic muscle)
   - Deep → condensed into **Buck's fascia** (deep fascia of penis); surrounds corpora cavernosa and corpus spongiosum; does not extend beyond the neck; separates superficial and deep dorsal veins
3. **Tunica albuginea**: fibrous envelope around corpora cavernosa only

### Prepuce (Foreskin)
- Fold of skin covering the glans
- Attached to the neck of the penis
- **Frenulum**: median fold on ventral surface from prepuce to external urethral meatus; highly sensitive
- **Preputial sac**: potential space between glans and prepuce

### Supports
- **Fundiform ligament**: from linea alba; splits to enclose proximal body; unites on urethral aspect with scrotal septum
- **Suspensory ligament**: deep to fundiform; triangular; from pubic symphysis to Buck's fascia

### Muscles at the Root

| Muscle | Action |
|--------|--------|
| **Bulbospongiosus** (x2) | Empties spongy urethra of residual semen/urine; anterior fibres maintain erection |
| **Ischiocavernosus** (x2) | Forces blood from cavernous spaces into corpora cavernosa; maintains erection |

### Blood Supply

**Arterial supply (3 sources):**
1. **Deep arteries of the penis**: from internal pudendal artery (branch of internal iliac); supply cavernous spaces via helicine arteries
2. **Bulbourethral artery**: from internal pudendal artery
3. **Superficial dorsal arteries**: from external pudendal arteries (branch of femoral artery)

**Venous drainage:**
- **Deep dorsal vein**: drains cavernous spaces → **prostatic venous plexus**
- **Superficial dorsal veins**: drain skin and cutaneous tissues

### Lymphatic Drainage
- **Glans penis** → deep inguinal lymph nodes (especially **node of Cloquet**)
- **Rest of penis** → superficial inguinal lymph nodes
- Important for metastasis of penile cancer

### Nerve Supply

| Type | Nerve/Source |
|------|-------------|
| **Sensory** | Dorsal nerve of penis; ilioinguinal nerve |
| **Motor** (root muscles) | Perineal branch of pudendal nerve (S2-S4) |
| **Autonomic** | Pelvic (inferior hypogastric) plexus via prostatic plexus |
| Parasympathetic | **Nervi erigentes** (S2-S4): vasodilator; mediates erection |
| Sympathetic | T11-L2: vasoconstrictor; mediates emission |

### Mechanism of Erection
- Purely **vascular phenomenon** in response to **parasympathetic stimulation**
- Sexual arousal → rapid inflow of blood from **helicine arteries** into cavernous spaces
- Filling compresses draining veins (with simultaneous venous sphincter spasm)
- Venous outflow occluded → increased pressure → inflation and rigidity of erectile tissue
- Pressure in corpora cavernosa reaches ~**100 mmHg** during erection
- **Tunica albuginea** contributes to stiffness
- Controlled by **nervi erigentes** (S2-S4)

### Mechanism of Ejaculation
Two processes:
1. **Emission** (sympathetic, T11-L2): seminal fluid from vas deferens, seminal vesicles, and prostate → prostatic urethra; internal urethral sphincter constricts to prevent retrograde flow
2. **Ejaculation** (somatic): rhythmic contraction of **bulbospongiosus** (perineal nerve, S2-S4) → propels fluid through penile urethra to exterior

**Neural controls differ**: erection = parasympathetic; ejaculation = sympathetic + somatic

### Clinical Correlations

| Condition | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Impotence** | Failure to achieve erection; causes: psychogenic disturbance, arterial insufficiency (atheroma), or nervi erigentes involvement (diabetes) |
| **Priapism** | Persistent erection; due to persistent venous sphincter spasm |
| **Peyronie's disease (chordee)** | Localised thickening/plaque of corpora cavernosa; penis curves during erection |
| **Phimosis** | Narrowing of distal prepuce; prevents retraction over glans; may interfere with micturition |
| **Paraphimosis** | Prepuce stuck on glans posteriorly during erection; interferes with copulation |
| **Circumcision** | Surgical removal of prepuce; for phimosis or religious reasons |
