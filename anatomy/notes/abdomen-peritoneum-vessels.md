# The Peritoneum, Peritoneal Cavity, Mesentery, and Abdominal Blood Vessels

---

## The Peritoneum

- **Double-layered membrane** in the abdomen
- Lines the abdominal cavity and covers the abdominal viscera
- Made of **mesothelium** (simple squamous epithelial cells)

### Functions

- Support the viscera
- Provide pathways for blood vessels and lymph to travel to and from viscera

### Two Layers

| Layer | Description |
|---|---|
| **Parietal peritoneum** | Lines the abdominopelvic wall; derived from **somatic mesoderm**; receives same somatic innervation as the abdominal wall — pain is **well-localised**; sensitive to pressure, pain, laceration, and temperature |
| **Visceral peritoneum** | Covers the abdominal viscera; derived from **splanchnic mesoderm**; same autonomic nerve supply as the viscera it covers — pain is **poorly localised**; only sensitive to **stretch and chemical irritation** |

> Visceral pain is referred to dermatomes supplied by the same spinal cord segments as the nerve fibers innervating the viscera.

---

## Peritoneal Cavity

- **Potential space** between parietal and visceral peritoneum
- Normally contains only a small amount of **lubricating fluid**

### Division

| Compartment | Contents |
|---|---|
| **Greater sac** | Majority of the peritoneal cavity; extends from diaphragm to pelvic cavity |
| **Lesser sac (omental bursa)** | Posterior to stomach and lesser omentum; communicates with greater sac via **epiploic foramen (of Winslow)** |

### Greater Sac Divisions

- Divided by the **transverse mesocolon**:
  - **Supracolic compartment** (above): stomach, liver, spleen
  - **Infracolic compartment** (below): small and large intestines

---

## Intraperitoneal vs Retroperitoneal Organs

### Intraperitoneal

- Enveloped by visceral peritoneum (anteriorly and posteriorly)
- Examples: **stomach, liver, spleen, jejunum, ileum, transverse colon, sigmoid colon**

### Retroperitoneal

- Only covered by parietal peritoneum on their anterior surface

#### Primarily Retroperitoneal (developed and remained outside parietal peritoneum)

- **Esophagus, rectum, kidneys, ureters, suprarenal glands**

#### Secondarily Retroperitoneal (initially intraperitoneal, mesentery fused with posterior wall)

- **Ascending and descending colon**, duodenum (except proximal 2 cm), pancreas (except tail)

### Mnemonic: **SAD PUCKER**

| Letter | Organ |
|---|---|
| **S** | Suprarenal (adrenal) glands |
| **A** | Aorta / IVC |
| **D** | Duodenum (except proximal 2 cm) |
| **P** | Pancreas (except tail) |
| **U** | Ureters |
| **C** | Colon (ascending and descending) |
| **K** | Kidneys |
| **E** | Esophagus |
| **R** | Rectum |

## Peritoneal Reflections

### Mesentery

- **Double layer of visceral peritoneum** connecting intraperitoneal organ to the posterior abdominal wall
- Pathway for nerves, blood vessels, and lymphatics
- **"The mesentery"** = mesentery of the small intestine
- Other mesenteries named by organ: **transverse mesocolon, sigmoid mesocolon, mesoappendix**

### Omentum

- Visceral peritoneum extending from stomach/proximal duodenum to other organs

#### Greater Omentum

- **Four layers** of visceral peritoneum
- Descends from **greater curvature** of stomach and proximal duodenum → folds back up → attaches to **anterior surface of transverse colon**
- Known as the **"abdominal policeman"** (role in immunity)

#### Lesser Omentum

- **Double layer** of visceral peritoneum
- From **lesser curvature** of stomach and proximal duodenum → to **liver**
- Two parts:
  - **Hepatogastric ligament**: flat, broad sheet
  - **Hepatoduodenal ligament**: free edge; contains the **portal triad** (portal vein, hepatic artery, bile duct)

### Peritoneal Ligaments

- Double folds of peritoneum connecting viscera together or viscera to the abdominal wall
- Examples: **hepatogastric ligament, gastrosplenic ligament, lienorenal (splenorenal) ligament**

### Peritoneal Pouches

- **Males**: rectovesical pouch (between rectum and bladder; usually empty)
- **Females**:
  - **Vesicouterine pouch** (between bladder and uterus; usually empty)
  - **Rectouterine pouch** (between rectum and uterus; contains terminal ileum coils)

### Referred Pain

| Origin | Referred to Region |
|---|---|
| **Foregut** (esophagus, stomach, pancreas, liver, gallbladder, proximal duodenum) | Epigastric region |
| **Midgut** (duodenum distal to bile duct to proximal 2/3 of transverse colon) | Umbilical region |
| **Hindgut** (distal 1/3 of transverse colon to upper anal canal) | Pubic/hypogastric region |
| **Retroperitoneal organs** (kidney, pancreas) | Back pain |
| **Diaphragmatic irritation** (liver, gallbladder, duodenum) | Shoulder tip pain |

## Abdominal Aorta

- Continuation of thoracic aorta beginning at **T12**
- Approximately **13 cm long**
- Ends at **L4** by bifurcating into **right and left common iliac arteries**

### Branches (Grouped)

#### Unpaired (Anterior)

| Branch | Level | Supply |
|---|---|---|
| **Coeliac trunk** | T12 | Foregut derivatives |
| **Superior mesenteric artery** | L1 | Midgut derivatives |
| **Inferior mesenteric artery** | L3 | Hindgut derivatives |
| **Median sacral artery** | Terminal bifurcation | Posterior abdominal wall |

#### Paired (Lateral/Posterolateral)

- Inferior phrenic arteries
- Middle suprarenal arteries
- **Renal arteries**
- **Gonadal (testicular/ovarian) arteries**
- Lumbar arteries (4 pairs)

### Coeliac Trunk (T12)

Arises from anterior aspect at aortic hiatus; extends ~1 cm; divides into three:

| Branch | Course and Key Branches |
|---|---|
| **Left gastric artery** | Smallest; ascends across diaphragm; gives esophageal branches; runs along lesser curvature; anastomoses with right gastric |
| **Splenic artery** | Tortuous course along superior margin of pancreas; runs in splenorenal ligament; terminates in 4-5 splenic branches; gives **left gastroepiploic**, **short gastric**, and **pancreatic branches** |
| **Common hepatic artery** | Only branch going right; sole arterial supply to liver; divides into **proper hepatic** and **gastroduodenal** |

**Proper hepatic artery** gives:
- **Right gastric** (pylorus and lesser curvature)
- **Right and left hepatic arteries** (liver lobes)
- **Cystic artery** (gallbladder; branch of right hepatic)

**Gastroduodenal artery** gives:
- **Right gastroepiploic** (greater curvature)
- **Superior pancreaticoduodenal** (head of pancreas and duodenum)

> Stomach receives arterial supply from all three branches of coeliac trunk via anastomoses along greater and lesser curvatures.

### Superior Mesenteric Artery (L1)

- Second major anterior branch; supplies **midgut** (from major duodenal papilla to proximal 2/3 of transverse colon)

**Relations:**
- **Anterior**: pyloric part of stomach, splenic vein, neck of pancreas
- **Posterior**: left renal vein, uncinate process, inferior duodenum

**Branches:**

| Branch | Supply |
|---|---|
| Inferior pancreaticoduodenal | Head of pancreas, uncinate process, duodenum |
| Jejunal and ileal arteries | Small intestine (arcades and vasa recta) |
| Middle colic artery | Transverse colon |
| Right colic artery | Ascending colon |
| Ileocolic artery | Cecum, appendix, ascending colon, terminal ileum |

> Jejunum: fewer arcades, longer vasa recta. Ileum: more arcades, shorter vasa recta.

### Inferior Mesenteric Artery (L3)

- Last major anterior branch; supplies **hindgut** (distal 1/3 of transverse colon to upper anal canal)

**Branches:**

| Branch | Supply |
|---|---|
| **Left colic artery** | Distal 1/3 of transverse colon, descending colon |
| **Sigmoid arteries** (2-4) | Descending and sigmoid colon |
| **Superior rectal artery** (continuation of IMA) | Rectum |

### Anastomoses of IMA with SMA

| Anastomosis | Description |
|---|---|
| **Marginal artery (of Drummond)** | Continuous arterial circle along inner border of colon; union of ileocolic, right colic, middle colic (SMA) with left colic and sigmoid (IMA) |
| **Arc of Riolan** | Anastomosis between middle colic (SMA) and left colic (IMA); less common |

> **Watershed area**: splenic flexure has dual blood supply from most distal branches of SMA and IMA — more sensitive to systemic hypoperfusion.

### Clinical Notes

- **Aortic aneurysm**: most common site is abdominal component; may compress nerve roots (lower limb pain/numbness)
- **Coeliac trunk compression syndrome**: compressed by median arcuate ligament → ischemia

## Inferior Vena Cava (IVC)

- Formed by union of **common iliac veins** at **L5**
- Ascends on posterior abdominal wall, **to the right of abdominal aorta**
- Pierces central tendon of diaphragm at **caval hiatus (T8)**
- Drains into **right atrium**

### Tributaries

| Tributary | Drains |
|---|---|
| Common iliac veins | Lower limbs and gluteal region |
| Lumbar veins | Posterior abdominal wall |
| **Renal veins** | Kidneys, left adrenal gland, left testis/ovary |
| **Right testicular/ovarian vein** | Right gonad (left drains into left renal vein) |
| **Right suprarenal vein** | Right adrenal gland (left drains into left renal vein) |
| Inferior phrenic veins | Diaphragm |
| **Hepatic veins** | Liver |

> No tributaries from spleen, pancreas, gallbladder, or abdominal GIT — these drain into the **portal system** first.

## Portal Venous System

- Carries nutrient-rich venous blood from GI tract and spleen to the **liver** for processing

### Portal Vein

- Formed **posterior to the neck of the pancreas** at **L2**
- Union of **splenic vein** and **superior mesenteric vein**
- Ascends behind superior part of duodenum and bile duct
- Divides into **right and left branches** before entering liver

### Superior Mesenteric Vein (SMV)

- Begins in right iliac fossa (convergence of veins from terminal ileum, cecum, appendix)
- Drains small intestine, cecum, ascending colon, transverse colon
- Ascends in mesentery → posterior to neck of pancreas → joins splenic vein

**Tributaries of SMV**: right gastro-omental vein, inferior pancreaticoduodenal veins, jejunal veins, ileal vein, ileocolic vein, right colic vein, middle colic vein

### Porto-Systemic Anastomoses

| Site | Portal Connection | Systemic Connection |
|---|---|---|
| **Esophageal** | Esophageal branch of left gastric vein | Esophageal tributaries of azygous system |
| **Rectal** | Superior rectal vein | Inferior rectal veins |
| **Paraumbilical** | Portal veins of liver | Veins of anterior abdominal wall |
| **Retroperitoneal** | Portal tributaries of mesenteric veins | Retroperitoneal veins |

### Clinical: Portal Hypertension

- **Liver cirrhosis** → scarring and fibrosis → obstructs portal vein → pressure rises
- Produces **dilated variceal veins** at anastomosis sites:
  - **Esophageal varices** (may rupture → fatal hemorrhage)
  - **Haemorrhoids** (rectal)
  - **Caput medusae** (paraumbilical)
- Treatment: **portacaval or splenorenal shunt** (diverts blood from portal to systemic system)
