# Female Reproductive System

The female reproductive system consists of the **ovaries**, **uterine tubes** (oviducts/fallopian tubes), **uterus**, **cervix**, **vagina**, and **external genitalia (vulva)**.

---

## Ovaries

### Location and General Features
- Small, ovoid/almond-shaped organs lying in the **pelvic cavity**
- Located on either side of the uterus
- Produce **oocytes** (eggs) and **sex hormones** (estrogen, progesterone)

### Coverings and Structure

| Layer | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Germinal epithelium** | Simple squamous to cuboidal mesothelium (modified peritoneum) covering the ovary |
| **Tunica albuginea** | Thick connective tissue capsule beneath the germinal epithelium |
| **Cortex** | Broad peripheral zone containing **follicles** at various stages embedded in loose connective tissue stroma |
| **Medulla** | Inner region containing nerves, blood vessels, lymphatics, loose CT, smooth muscle, and **rete ovarii** (solid cellular cords) |
| **Hilum** | Entry/exit point for vessels and nerves |

### Ligaments

| Ligament | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **Suspensory ligament** | From ovary to pelvic wall; contains ovarian vessels |
| **Ovarian ligament** | From ovary to lateral uterus (near uterine tube) |
| **Mesovarium** | Part of broad ligament; attaches ovary to posterior surface of broad ligament |
| **Broad ligament** | Double fold of peritoneum supporting uterus and adnexa |

### Blood Supply
- **Ovarian artery**: direct branch of the **abdominal aorta** (runs retroperitoneally)
- **Ovarian artery anastomoses** with uterine artery

### Venous Drainage
- **Right ovarian vein** → IVC
- **Left ovarian vein** → left renal vein

### Lymphatic Drainage
- **Para-aortic lymph nodes** (following ovarian arteries)

### Nerve Supply
- Sympathetic: T10-T11 (via ovarian plexus)
- Parasympathetic: S2-S4

## Ovarian Follicles

### Follicular Development

| Stage | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Primordial follicle** | Unilaminar, resting; primary oocyte surrounded by single layer of **simple squamous** epithelium |
| **Primary follicle** | Unilaminar, growing; primary oocyte surrounded by single layer of **simple cuboidal** epithelium |
| **Early secondary follicle (preantral)** | Multilaminar; oocyte surrounded by **zona pellucida** and stratified granulosa cells |
| **Late secondary follicle (antral)** | Multilaminar; **antrum** forms (containing follicular fluid/liquor folliculi); outer **theca interna** develops |
| **Graafian follicle (mature/tertiary)** | Mature follicle; large antrum; oocyte in **cumulus oophorus** surrounded by **corona radiata** (innermost columnar granulosa cells); theca interna and theca externa layers; **Call-Exner bodies** (PAS-positive inclusions in granulosa cells) |

### Key Structures in Mature Follicle
- **Zona pellucida**: acellular glycoprotein layer surrounding oocyte
- **Stratum granulosum**: parietal follicular lining of polyhedral granulosa cells
- **Cumulus oophorus**: cluster of granulosa cells surrounding the oocyte
- **Corona radiata**: innermost columnar cells immediately around the oocyte
- **Theca interna**: vascular; synthesises **androstenedione** (converted to estrogen by granulosa cells under FSH)
- **Theca externa**: fibrous outer layer; merges with ovarian stroma

### Ovulation
- Mature Graafian follicle ruptures, releasing the **secondary oocyte**
- Occurs around day 14 of the menstrual cycle

### Corpus Luteum Formation
1. After ovulation, follicle ruptures and collapses
2. **Corpus hemorrhagicum**: blood fills the antrum
3. Granulosa cells enlarge and luteinize → **large luteal cells**
4. Theca interna cells → **small luteal cells**
5. Forms the **corpus luteum**

### Corpus Luteum Secretions
- **Progesterone**: prepares uterine lining
- **Estrogens**: work with progesterone
- **Relaxin**: relaxes uterine muscles and pubic symphysis
- **Inhibin**: decreases FSH and LH secretion

### Corpus Luteum Regression
- Regresses if no pregnancy occurs
- Luteal pigment condenses → reddish → fibrosis → resorption
- Remaining scar = **corpus albicans** ("white body")

### Follicular Atresia
- Most follicles and oocytes degenerate as female matures
- Occurs from before birth until menopause
- Particularly intense after birth (cessation of maternal hormones), during puberty, and during pregnancy

## Uterine Tubes (Oviducts/Fallopian Tubes)

### General Features
- About **12 cm long**
- Highly mobile due to smooth muscle in wall
- Not physically connected to the ovary; fimbriae sweep oocyte into tube

### Parts (from uterus to ovary)

| Part | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Intramural (interstitial)** | Portion within the uterine wall; opens into uterus |
| **Isthmus** | Narrow portion adjacent to uterus |
| **Ampulla** | More dilated; extends to infundibulum; **most common site of fertilisation** |
| **Infundibulum** | Funnel-shaped; near ovary; has **fimbriae** (finger-like projections) |

### Wall Structure

1. **Mucosa**: longitudinal folds (most numerous in ampulla); simple columnar epithelium with:
   - **Ciliated cells**: cilia move fluid toward uterus; prevent bacteria entering peritoneal cavity; some ciliary tracts beat opposite direction to assist sperm transport
   - **Secretory (peg) cells**: secrete mucus
2. **Muscularis**: inner circular and outer longitudinal smooth muscle; only inner circular is well developed
3. **Serosa**: connective tissue with blood vessels and nerves; covered by mesothelium

### Blood Supply
- **Uterine artery** (branch of internal iliac artery)
- **Tubal branches of ovarian artery**

### Lymphatic Drainage
- Para-aortic and internal iliac lymph nodes

## Uterus

### General Features
- Pear-shaped, hollow, muscular organ
- Site of **fetal development**
- About 7.5 cm long, 5 cm wide, 2.5 cm thick (nulliparous)

### Parts

| Part | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Fundus** | Dome-shaped portion above the level of uterine tube openings |
| **Body** | Main portion; between fundus and cervix |
| **Uterine horns** | Lateral projections where fallopian tubes attach |
| **Cervix** | Inferior narrow portion projecting into vagina |

### Position and Orientation

**Normal position (anteversion and anteflexion):**
- **Anteversion**: uterus tilted forward so the long axis of the cervix is at ~90° to the long axis of the vagina (the entire uterus is tilted anteriorly)
- **Anteflexion**: the body of the uterus is bent forward relative to the cervix

### Ligaments of the Uterus

| Ligament | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **Broad ligament** | Double fold of peritoneum; supports uterus, tubes, ovaries |
| **Round ligament** | From uterine horn to labia majora via inguinal canal; maintains anteversion |
| **Cardinal (transverse cervical) ligament** | From cervix to lateral pelvic wall; main support of uterus |
| **Uterosacral ligament** | From cervix to sacrum; supports posterior aspect |
| **Pubocervical ligament** | From cervix to pubis |

### Layers of the Uterine Wall

#### 1. Endometrium (Innermost — Mucosa)
- Simple columnar epithelium (with or without cilia)
- Thick **lamina propria**: rich in cells, collagen, straight and coiled (**helicine**) arteries
- Tubular **uterine glands** extending deep into lamina propria

**Two layers:**

| Layer | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Lamina functionalis (decidua)** | Contains uterine glands and helicine arteries; sloughed off at end of each menstrual cycle; regenerates from basalis; site of embryo implantation |
| **Lamina basalis** | Permanent portion; contains straight arteries; provides oxygen and nutrients to the functionalis |

#### 2. Myometrium (Thickest Layer)
- Smooth muscle; inner circular and outer longitudinal layers
- **Stratum vasculare** between the two muscular layers
- Increases in thickness during pregnancy via **hyperplasia** (increased cell number) and **hypertrophy** (increased cell size)
- Decreases after pregnancy

#### 3. Perimetrium (Outermost — Serosa)
- Thin outermost layer
- Connective tissue with blood/lymph vessels, nerves, sympathetic ganglia
- Covered by **mesothelium** of the peritoneal cavity

### Blood Supply
- **Uterine artery** (branch of internal iliac artery): main supply
- Anastomoses with ovarian artery

### Venous Drainage
- Uterine veins → internal iliac veins

### Lymphatic Drainage
- Body: **external iliac lymph nodes**
- Cervix: **internal iliac and sacral lymph nodes**

### Nerve Supply
- **Hypogastric plexus** (sympathetic T10-L2)
- **Pelvic splanchnic nerves** (parasympathetic S2-S4)

### Relations

| Surface | Relation |
|---------|----------|
| **Anterior** | Urinary bladder; vesicouterine pouch |
| **Posterior** | Rectum; rectouterine pouch (of Douglas) |
| **Lateral** | Broad ligament, uterine artery, ureter ("water under the bridge") |
| **Superior** | Intestinal loops |
| **Inferior** | Vagina (cervix projects into it) |

## Cervix

### General Features
- Tapering portion of the uterus projecting into the vaginal canal
- Contains the **cervical canal (os)**

### Parts
- **Supravaginal portion**: above vaginal attachment
- **Vaginal portion (portio vaginalis)**: projects into vagina; has anterior and posterior lips

### Cervical Canal
- Connects uterine cavity to vagina
- **Internal os**: opening into uterus
- **External os**: opening into vagina

### Structure
- Wall differs from rest of uterus: mainly **dense collagenous and elastic fibres** (~85%)
- Only ~**15% smooth muscle**
- Mucosa contains **mucous glands** and is thrown into deep branching folds called **plicae palmatae**
- Undergoes tremendous dilation during birth

### Clinical
- Site of **cervical screening** (Pap smear) for detecting dysplasia/cancer
- **Cervical ectropion**: columnar epithelium extends onto vaginal portion

## Vagina

### General Features
- Fibromuscular tube; extends from cervix to vestibule
- Functions: birth canal, receives penis during intercourse, passage for menstrual flow

### Wall Structure (3 layers)

#### 1. Mucosa
- **Non-keratinised stratified squamous epithelium** (contains small amounts of keratin)
- **No glands** — fluid secretions during arousal come from blood plasma seeping through the epithelium from engorged venous plexus
- **Lamina propria**: loose CT; rich in elastic fibres (important for dilation during birth); highly vascularised

#### 2. Muscular Layer
- Mainly **longitudinal** smooth muscle bundles
- Some **circular** bundles near mucosa

#### 3. Adventitia
- Dense connective tissue merging with surrounding structures
- Rich in **elastic fibres** (important for birth dilation)
- Extensive blood vessels, nerve plexus, and nerve cells

### Rugae
- Transverse folds of the vaginal mucosa
- Allow expansion during intercourse and childbirth

### Fornices
- Recesses formed by the projection of the cervix into the vagina
- **Anterior fornix**: in front of cervix; shallow
- **Posterior fornix**: behind cervix; deeper; related to rectouterine pouch (Pouch of Douglas)
- **Lateral fornices**: on either side

### Blood Supply
- **Vaginal artery** (branch of internal iliac artery; or from uterine artery)
- Anastomoses along the vagina forming a longitudinal chain

### Nerve Supply
- **Inferior hypogastric plexus** (sympathetic + parasympathetic)
- Lower part: **pudendal nerve** (somatic, S2-S4) for pain sensation

### Lymphatic Drainage
- **Upper third**: external iliac lymph nodes
- **Middle third**: internal iliac lymph nodes
- **Lower third**: superficial inguinal lymph nodes

## External Genitalia (Vulva)

The vulva includes:
- **Mons pubis**: rounded fatty prominence over the pubic symphysis; covered with hair after puberty
- **Labia majora**: larger outer folds; continuous with mons pubis; contain sebaceous and sweat glands; homologue of the scrotum
- **Labia minora**: smaller inner folds; no fat; rich in blood vessels and nerve endings; enclose the **vestibule**
- **Vestibule**: area enclosed by labia minora; contains openings of:
  - Vaginal orifice (with hymen)
  - External urethral orifice
  - Ducts of greater vestibular (Bartholin's) glands
  - Ducts of lesser vestibular glands
- **Clitoris**: erectile organ; homologue of the penis; composed of two corpora cavernosa; highly sensitive; glans clitoris covered by prepuce
- **Greater vestibular (Bartholin's) glands**: homologue of bulbourethral glands; secrete mucus into vestibule
- **Vestibular bulbs**: erectile tissue on either side of vaginal orifice; homologue of bulb of penis

## Perineum in Females

### Superficial Perineal Pouch Contents
- Bulb of the vestibule (erectile tissue)
- Crura of clitoris
- Bulbospongiosus muscle (surrounds vaginal orifice)
- Ischiocavernosus muscles (pair; surround crura)
- Superficial transverse perineal muscle
- Greater vestibular (Bartholin's) glands
- Internal pudendal artery branches
- Perineal branch of pudendal nerve

### Deep Perineal Pouch Contents
- Urethra
- External urethral sphincter (voluntary)
- Vagina
- Deep transverse perineal muscle
- Homologues of bulbourethral glands

### Perineal Body
- Fibromuscular midline structure between vaginal orifice and anal canal
- Point of attachment for: bulbospongiosus, superficial transverse perineal, external anal sphincter, levator ani
- **Critical for pelvic floor integrity** — damage during childbirth can lead to pelvic organ prolapse

### Blood Supply
- Internal pudendal artery (via perineal artery)
- Superficial and deep external pudendal arteries

### Nerve Supply
- Pudendal nerve (S2-S4)
- Perineal branch of posterior femoral cutaneous nerve
