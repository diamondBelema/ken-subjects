# Pelvis: Bony Pelvis, Joints, Measurements & Pelvic Floor

## Introduction

The **pelvis** (L. *basin*) is the region of the trunk inferoposterior to the abdomen, forming the transition between the trunk and the lower limbs.

- **Anatomically**: the part surrounded by the pelvic girdle (bony pelvis)
- **Topographically**: a bony and ligamentous framework lined by soft tissue, closed inferiorly by the **pelvic floor** (muscle and fascia)
- The **perineum** lies below the pelvic floor

---

## Bony Pelvis

The bony pelvis is composed of **4 bones** connected by **4 joints** and lined by **4 muscles**:

| Bones | Joints |
|-------|--------|
| 2 hip (pelvic) bones | 2 sacroiliac joints |
| 1 sacrum | 1 pubic symphysis |
| 1 coccyx | 1 sacrococcygeal joint |

**Functions of the bony pelvis:**
- Protects pelvic viscera
- Supports body weight when sitting/standing (transfers weight from axial to appendicular skeleton)
- Provides attachment for muscles
- In females, provides bony support for the **birth canal**

---

### Hip Bone (Os Coxae)

Formed by the fusion of **3 bones** at the **acetabulum**:

#### Pubis
- Angulated bone forming the **anterior** part of the pelvic bone
- **Body** forms 1/5 of the acetabulum
- **Symphyseal surface** unites with the opposite side at the pubic symphysis
- Superior and inferior rami participate in the **obturator foramen**
- Inferior ramus fuses with ischial ramus to form the **ischiopubic ramus**
- Key landmarks: pubic crest, pubic tubercle, pecten pubis (pectineal line), subpubic angle

#### Ischium
- V-shaped, forms the **posteroinferior** part of the pelvic bone
- **Ischial tuberosity**: roughened projection; site of attachment for sacrotuberous ligament, origin of hamstring muscles, inferior gemellus, quadratus femoris
- **Ischial spine**: separates the lesser sciatic notch (below) from the greater sciatic notch (above)
- Parts: body, superior ramus, inferior ramus, spine, tuberosity

#### Ilium
- Most **superior** portion of the hip bone
- **Iliac crest**: runs between ASIS and PSIS
- Inner aspect bears the **auricular surface** (articulates with sacrum)
- Parts: ala, body, iliac crest, iliac fossa
- Landmarks: ASIS, AIIS, PSIS, PIIS

---

### Sacrum

- Wedge-shaped bone composed of **5 fused sacral vertebrae**
- Located between the hip bones; forms the **roof and posterosuperior wall** of the posterior pelvic cavity

**Key features:**
- **Base**: superior surface of S1; anterior projecting edge is the **sacral promontory**
- **Apex**: inferior oval facet; articulates with the coccyx
- **Anterior sacral foramina** (4 pairs): larger; transmit ventral rami of sacral spinal nerves
- **Posterior sacral foramina** (4 pairs): transmit dorsal rami
- **Sacral canal**: continuation of vertebral canal; contains cauda equina
- **Sacral hiatus**: absence of laminae and spinous process of S5 (sometimes S4); site for **caudal anesthesia**; bounded by **sacral cornua**
- **Lumbosacral angle**: articulation of sacrum with L5; ranges 130°-160°

**Dorsal surface crests:**
- Median sacral crest (fused spinous processes of S1-S4)
- Intermediate sacral crests (fused articular processes)
- Lateral sacral crests (tips of transverse processes)

**Clinical notes:**
- Dura and arachnoid mater end at **S2**
- **Sacralization**: transverse process of L5 fuses with sacrum (congenital)
- **Lumbarization**: S1 not completely fused, appears as extra lumbar vertebra

---

### Coccyx

- Vestigial tail bone; **4 fused vertebrae** forming a small triangular bone
- Base articulates with the lower end of the sacrum
- During childbirth, coccyx moves backwards (nodding) at the sacrococcygeal joint to increase pelvic outlet space

---

## Division of the Pelvis

An imaginary plane through the **linea terminalis** (pelvic brim) divides the pelvis into:

### Greater (False/Major) Pelvis
- Lies **above** the linea terminalis
- Posteriorly: L5 vertebra
- Laterally: iliac fossae
- Anteriorly: lower anterior abdominal wall (where ilium is deficient)
- Considered part of the **abdominal cavity**
- Supports ileum, caecum, appendix, and sigmoid colon

### Lesser (True/Minor) Pelvis
- Lies **inferior and posterior** to the linea terminalis
- Has a **pelvic inlet** (superior aperture), **pelvic cavity**, and **pelvic outlet** (inferior aperture)
- Contains rectum, bladder, and internal genitalia
- In females, modified to serve as the **birth canal**

---

## Linea Terminalis (Pelvic Brim)

Formed by (posterior to anterior):
1. Anterior border of the base of S1 (sacral promontory + margin of ala)
2. Arcuate line of the ilium
3. Pectineal line of the pubis

---

## Pelvic Inlet (Superior Aperture)

Formed by:
- **Posteriorly**: anterior surface of the base of S1
- **Laterally**: arcuate line and pectineal line
- **Anteriorly**: pubic crest and anterior continuation of pectineal line (pectin pubis)

---

## Pelvic Cavity

- Continuation of the abdominal cavity through the pelvic inlet
- Extends from pelvic brim above to pelvic outlet below
- Almost rounded in shape
- **Borders**: symphysis pubis (anterior), sacral hollow (posterior), soft tissues (lateral)
- A short, curved canal, deeper posteriorly than anteriorly

---

## Pelvic Outlet (Inferior Aperture)

Diamond-shaped; bounded by:
- **Posteriorly**: tip of coccyx
- **Laterally**: ischial tuberosities
- **Posterolaterally**: sacrotuberous ligament
- **Anteriorly**: pubic arch

The terminal parts of the urinary tract, gastrointestinal tract, and vagina pass through the pelvic outlet.

---

## Pelvic Measurements (Pelvimetry)

Critical in obstetrics for predicting vaginal delivery likelihood.

### Pelvic Inlet Diameters

| Diameter | Landmarks | Measurement |
|----------|-----------|-------------|
| **True conjugate** | Sacral promontory → superior margin of pubic symphysis | 11–11.5 cm (F); 10 cm (M) |
| **Diagonal conjugate** | Sacral promontory → inferior margin of pubic symphysis | ~12 cm |
| **Obstetric conjugate** | Sacral promontory → point few mm below superior margin of symphysis | ~10.5 cm |
| **Anatomical transverse** | Widest distance across pelvic brim | 13–13.5 cm (F); 12.5 cm (M) |
| **Obstetric transverse** | Bisects the true conjugate; slightly shorter than anatomical transverse | — |
| **Oblique (R & L)** | Sacroiliac joint → contralateral iliopectineal eminence | ~12 cm each |
| **Sacrocotyloid** | Midpoint of sacral promontory → ipsilateral iliopubic eminence | ~9.5 cm |

**Key clinical points:**
- **Obstetric conjugate** is the shortest AP diameter the fetal head must pass through
- Cannot be measured directly; estimated by subtracting **1.5–2 cm** from diagonal conjugate
- If obstetric conjugate < **10 cm** → **contracted pelvic inlet**
- The **anatomical transverse** is the largest diameter of the pelvic inlet

### Pelvic Cavity Diameters

- The cavity is almost **circular** in shape
- Anterior wall (pubic bones + symphysis): depth **4 cm**
- Posterior wall (curve of sacrum): length **12 cm**
- All diameters are considered approximately **12 cm**

### Pelvic Outlet Diameters

| Diameter | Measurement |
|----------|-------------|
| AP diameter | 13 cm (lower border of symphysis → tip of coccyx) |
| Oblique diameter | 12 cm (R or L sacrospinous ligament → contralateral obturator foramen) |
| Transverse diameter | 11 cm (between ischial spines) |

The pelvic outlet is **slightly smaller** than the inlet.

---

## Orientation of the Pelvis

- In anatomical position, the **ASIS** lies on the same vertical plane as the top of the pubic symphysis
- In living females, the ASIS is tilted slightly forward → greater lumbar curvature and more prominent buttocks

---

## Pelvic Types (Caldwell-Moloy Classification)

| Type | Frequency | Key Features |
|------|-----------|-------------|
| **Gynaecoid** | 50% | "Female" pelvis; wider brim; blunt ischial spines; subpubic angle up to 90°; wide subpubic arch (fits 4 knuckles/fist); **most suitable for childbirth** |
| **Anthropoid** | 25% | Oval shape; shorter transverse diameter; favors posterior fetal position; seen in tall women with narrow shoulders |
| **Android** | 20% | "Male" pelvis; heart-shaped brim; shorter AP diameter; wider transverse diameter; **childbirth is difficult** |
| **Platypelloid** | 5% | Rare; kidney-shaped brim; shorter AP diameter; wider transverse; **not conducive for vaginal delivery** |

### Other Variants
- **Symmetrically contracted pelvis**: small pelvis with symmetrical shape
- **Rachitic pelvis**: caused by rickets; sacrum rotated so promontory projects forward and coccyx tips backward; AP inlet narrowed, outlet increased
- **Asymmetrical pelvis**: due to scoliosis, poliomyelitis, pelvic fracture, congenital abnormality

---

## Sex Differentiation of the Pelvis

| Feature | Male | Female |
|---------|------|--------|
| Pubic arch | Narrower (due to crus of penis) | Wider (due to clitoris) |
| Acetabulum | Larger | Smaller |
| Distance: acetabulum–ilium–symphysis | Shorter | Longer |
| Sacral facet base : ala ratio | Larger : smaller | Smaller : larger |
| **Subpubic angle** | Smaller (< 70°) | Larger (~90°) |

## Pelvic Joints

### Pubic Symphysis
- **Secondary cartilaginous joint**
- Articular surfaces covered with **hyaline cartilage**
- **Fibrocartilaginous disc** between the surfaces
- A cavity may develop in the disc (not lined with synovial membrane)
- Very little movement normally

### Sacroiliac Joint
- **Modified synovial plane joint**
- Rough articular surfaces
- Bound by **anterior and posterior sacroiliac ligaments** (among the strongest in the body)
- Almost **immobile**, except during pregnancy

**Accessory ligaments:**
- Sacrotuberous ligament
- Sacrospinous ligament
- Iliolumbar ligament
- Sacrotuberous and sacrospinous ligaments convert greater and lesser sciatic notches into **foramina**

### Sacrococcygeal Joint
- Base of coccyx articulates with the tip of the sacrum
- During delivery, coccyx moves backwards (**nodding**) to increase space
- Fracture or arthritis causes **coccygodynia/coccydynia** (painful condition)

### Movement During Pregnancy
- Non-pregnant: very little movement
- Pregnancy hormones soften ligaments → more movement
- Provides more room for the fetal head
- Symphysis pubis may separate slightly; if excessive → pain on walking

## Pelvic Floor and Diaphragm

The pelvic cavity is closed inferiorly by the **pelvic diaphragm**, a layer of muscle and fascia forming the pelvic floor.

### Levator Ani
- The main component of the pelvic diaphragm
- Broad, thin muscle; slopes downward and medially
- Supports pelvic viscera and resists increases in intra-abdominal pressure
- Three parts:
  - **Pubococcygeus**: from pubis to coccyx
  - **Iliococcygeus**: from ischial spine to coccyx
  - **Puborectalis**: forms a U-shaped sling around the anorectal junction

### Coccygeus (Ischiococcygeus)
- Small triangular muscle posterior to levator ani
- From ischial spine to sacrum/coccyx
- Completes the posterior part of the pelvic diaphragm

---

## Pelvic Wall Muscles

### Obturator Internus
- **Origin**: ilium, ischium, deep surface of obturator membrane
- **Insertion**: greater trochanter of femur
- **Nerve supply**: nerve to obturator internus (L5, S1, S2)
- **Action**: abduction of flexed hip; lateral rotation

### Piriformis
- Triangular muscle; important landmark in the gluteal region
- **Origin**: anterior surface of S2-S4, superior margin of greater sciatic notch, sacrotuberous ligament
- **Insertion**: greater trochanter of femur (just above obturator internus insertion)
- **Nerve supply**: branches from ventral rami of L5, S1-S3
- **Action**: lateral rotation and abduction of femur

---

## Perineum Overview

- The perineum lies **below the pelvic floor**
- The **urogenital diaphragm** (perineal membrane) is a layer of fascia and muscle that contributes to support in the perineal region
- The root of the penis is situated in the **superficial perineal pouch** and is attached to the inferior aspect of the urogenital diaphragm
