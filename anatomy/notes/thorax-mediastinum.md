# The Thoracic Mediastinum

---

## Definition and Boundaries

- **Central compartment** of the thoracic cavity
- The space **between the pleural cavities** (each lung lies in its own pleural cavity)
- Extends from the **superior thoracic aperture** to the **inferior thoracic aperture (diaphragm)**
- Contains all thoracic viscera **except the lungs**
- Loose connective tissue holds structures together
- Directly continuous with the neck through the thoracic inlet — infections can spread between head, neck, thorax, abdomen, pelvis, and perineum

### Boundaries

| Direction | Structure |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Anterior** | Manubrium, sternum, costal cartilages |
| **Posterior** | Thoracic vertebrae and ribs |
| **Lateral** | Mediastinal pleura (medial borders of pleural sacs) |
| **Superior** | Thoracic inlet |
| **Inferior** | Diaphragm |

---

## Divisions

The **transverse thoracic plane** (passes from 2nd costal cartilage to lower border of T4 vertebra) divides the mediastinum into:

### 4-Compartment Model

| Division | Location | Key Contents |
|----------|----------|--------------|
| **Superior mediastinum** | Above transverse thoracic plane (T4/5) | Great vessels, trachea, esophagus, thymus |
| **Anterior mediastinum** | In front of pericardium | Thymus, internal thoracic vessels, lymph nodes |
| **Middle mediastinum** | Contains pericardium and heart | Heart, pericardium, ascending aorta, pulmonary trunk, phrenic nerves |
| **Posterior mediastinum** | Behind pericardium, in front of T5-T12 | Descending aorta, esophagus, azygos veins, thoracic duct |

### Key Notes
- The transverse thoracic plane passes through the **bifurcation of the trachea**, the **concavity of the aortic arch**, and just above the **bifurcation of the thoracic aorta**
- The manubriosternal joint lies opposite T4-T5 intervertebral disc
- Anterior and posterior mediastina are in **direct continuity** with the superior mediastinum
- Some structures in the superior mediastinum pass into the posterior mediastinum without going through the middle mediastinum

## Superior Mediastinum

### Boundaries
- **Superior**: thoracic inlet
- **Anterior**: dorsal surface of manubrium
- **Posterior**: ventral surfaces of T1-T4 vertebral bodies
- **Inferior**: continuous with inferior mediastinum at transverse thoracic plane
- **Lateral**: medial borders of pleural sacs (mediastinal pleura)

### Contents

#### Great Vessels
- Arch of aorta
- Brachiocephalic trunk
- Left common carotid artery
- Left subclavian artery
- Superior vena cava
- Brachiocephalic veins
- Azygos system of veins

#### Nerves
- Right and left vagus nerves
- Left recurrent laryngeal nerve
- Phrenic nerves
- Cardiac nerves and plexus
- Sympathetic trunk

#### Other Structures
- Trachea
- Esophagus
- Thoracic duct
- Thymus (most anterior)
- Sternohyoid and sternothyroid muscles

### Key Relationships
- **Thymus** lies most anteriorly
- **Brachiocephalic veins** cross anterior to arteries
- **Arch of aorta** arches over left main bronchus, superior to pulmonary trunk bifurcation
- **Trachea** (with trachealis muscle posteriorly) is anterior to esophagus
- **Thoracic duct** is on the right, posterior to esophagus, between aorta (left) and azygos vein (right)

---

## Anterior Mediastinum

### Boundaries
- **Anterior**: sternum
- **Posterior**: pericardium
- **Superior**: continuous with superior mediastinum (inferior part)
- **Inferior**: diaphragm

### Contents
- Internal thoracic artery and veins
- Prepericardial lymph nodes
- Parasternal and anterior mediastinal lymph nodes
- Sternopericardial ligaments
- Fat with loose connective tissue
- Small branches of internal thoracic arteries
- Thymus in infants and children (fatty remnants in adults)

### Key Relationships
- Lies anterior to heart and pericardium
- Communicates superiorly with inferior part of superior mediastinum
- Lies inferior to manubrium
- **Clinical relevance**: Common site for lymphadenopathy or retrosternal thyroid goiters extending inferiorly

---

## Middle Mediastinum

### Boundaries
- **Anterior**: anterior mediastinum (pericardium)
- **Posterior**: posterior mediastinum (pericardium)
- **Superior**: transverse thoracic plane (T4/5)
- **Inferior**: diaphragm
- **Lateral**: mediastinal pleura

### Contents
- **Heart and pericardium**
- Ascending aorta
- Pulmonary trunk and arteries
- Superior vena cava (lower part)
- Azygos vein (arches over right lung root)
- Pulmonary veins
- Main bronchi (right and left)
- Phrenic nerves
- Pericardiacophrenic vessels
- Lymph nodes (tracheobronchial, hilar)

### Key Relationships
- Ascending aorta is anterior and to the right of pulmonary trunk
- Pulmonary trunk arises from right ventricle, bifurcates into right and left pulmonary arteries at T5
- SVC lies to the right of ascending aorta
- Phrenic nerves descend anterior to lung roots, embedded in fibrous pericardium
- Main bronchi lie posterior to pulmonary vessels and SVC
- Continuous superiorly with inferior part of superior mediastinum

---

## Posterior Mediastinum

### Boundaries
- **Anterior**: pericardium and diaphragm
- **Posterior**: bodies of T5-T12 vertebrae
- **Superior**: transverse thoracic plane
- **Inferior**: diaphragm
- **Lateral**: mediastinal pleura

### Contents
- **Descending thoracic aorta**
- **Esophagus**
- **Azygos vein**
- Hemiazygos and accessory hemiazygos veins
- **Thoracic duct**
- Sympathetic trunks and splanchnic nerves
- Posterior mediastinal lymph nodes

### Key Relationships
- Esophagus lies anterior to thoracic aorta, slightly right in upper thorax, shifts left as it descends
- Thoracic aorta lies to left of vertebral column and esophagus
- Azygos vein ascends on right of median plane, arches over right lung root to join SVC
- Hemiazygos and accessory hemiazygos veins lie on left, cross midline to drain into azygos
- Thoracic duct lies between thoracic aorta (left) and azygos vein (right), posterior to esophagus
- Vagal fibers form esophageal plexus around esophagus
- Splanchnic nerves emerge from thoracic sympathetic trunk, descend obliquely along vertebral bodies to pierce diaphragm

## Mediastinal Structures in Detail

### Thoracic Aorta
- Descending part is continuation of arch
- Courses down in posterior mediastinum
- Crosses to left of vertebral column
- Passes through aortic hiatus of diaphragm at T12 to become abdominal aorta
- **Branches**: bronchial, esophageal, posterior intercostal, subcostal, superior phrenic arteries

### Azygos System of Veins
- Drains venous blood from body walls and mediastinal viscera to SVC
- **Azygos vein**: formed by union of right lumbar and right subcostal veins. Passes through aortic opening in diaphragm, ascends on posterior chest wall to T4, arches over right lung root to enter SVC
- **Hemiazygos vein**: arises on left, similar origin. Extends to T9, passes diagonally behind aorta and thoracic duct to drain into azygos at T8
- **Accessory hemiazygos vein**: crosses to right to drain into azygos at T7

### Esophagus in Thorax
- Begins at C6 (cricoid cartilage), slightly left of midline
- Passes through superior then posterior mediastina
- Swings back to midline at T5
- Passes downward and forward to enter esophageal opening in diaphragm at T10
- **Structure**: outer areolar tissue, inner stratified squamous epithelium, submucous layer, double muscular layer (outer longitudinal, inner circular)
- Upper two-thirds: striated muscle; lower third: smooth muscle

### Thoracic Duct
- Largest lymph vessel in the body
- Carries lymph from cisterna chyli in abdomen
- Passes through aortic hiatus to enter thorax
- Lies on bodies of T6-T12
- Ascends in midline to the right until T5, then crosses to left behind esophagus
- Passes upward in superior mediastinum, arches over apex of left lung and pleura
- Enters left brachiocephalic vein in neck
- Receives tributaries from all except right superior body quadrant

### Thymus Gland
- Important component of lymphatic system
- Lies behind manubrium (superior mediastinum)
- Can extend inferiorly to 4th costal cartilage in anterior mediastinum

### Sympathetic Trunk
- Paired nerve bundles on each paravertebral gutter
- Lies in posterior mediastinum
- Gives rise to lower thoracic splanchnic nerves
- **Stellate ganglion** lies on neck of first rib — tumors at lung apex may compress it causing **Horner's syndrome** (miosis, ptosis, dry face)

### Phrenic and Vagus Nerves
- Enter thorax between subclavian vein and subclavian artery (vagus medial)
- **Left phrenic**: passes inferiorly on pericardium of left ventricle, through central tendon of diaphragm
- **Left vagus**: runs with left common carotid, crosses arch of aorta, gives off left recurrent laryngeal nerve (loops under arch posterior to ligamentum arteriosum, ascends in tracheoesophageal groove)
- **Left vagus passes posteriorly, left phrenic remains anterior** — they cross each other on arch of aorta
- Left superior intercostal vein lies between the two nerves on the arch

## Clinical Correlations

### Mediastinitis
- Inflammation of connective tissue of mediastinum
- May arise from within or from neck
- Involves retropharyngeal and pretracheal spaces
- Infections from neck may track through retropharyngeal space to posterior mediastinum

### Mediastinal Widening
- Occurs in:
  - Hemorrhage (ruptured aorta in RTA, syphilis)
  - Neoplasia
  - Heart failure (CCF)
  - Cardiomyopathy

### Mediastinal Shift
- Shifts to **diseased side** in lung collapse
- Shifts to **healthy side** in pneumothorax or pyothorax
- Position of trachea at jugular notch is altered

### Mediastinal Syndrome
- Collection of symptoms with common etiology
- Observed in: enlarged mediastinal lymph nodes, bronchogenic carcinoma, aortic aneurysm

### Spread of Infection
- Infection from neck may track in front of anterior mediastinum
- Infection behind pre-vertebral fascia is trapped in superior mediastinum in front of vertebral bodies
- Purulent matter may track from neck through retropharyngeal space to posterior mediastinum

### Relationship with Lung Positions
- Pericardium covers left ventricle on left, overlies right atrium on right
- Internal thoracic artery sends anterior intercostal arteries into each intercostal space
- Central tendon of diaphragm descends to middle of xiphoid process and IVD between T9/T10
