# Pelvis & Perineum: Perineum

## Introduction

The **perineum** is the region of the body inferior to the pelvic floor. It is the area between the pubic symphysis anteriorly, the coccyx posteriorly, and the ischial tuberosities laterally.

---

## Boundaries and Landmarks

- **Anterior**: pubic arch and pubic symphysis
- **Posterior**: tip of the coccyx
- **Laterally**: ischial tuberosities and sacrotuberous ligaments
- The **perineal body** (central tendon of the perineum) is a fibromuscular midline structure at the center of the perineum, between the urogenital and anal triangles

---

## Divisions of the Perineum

An imaginary line drawn between the two **ischial tuberosities** divides the perineum into two triangles:

### Urogenital Triangle (Anterior)
- Contains the external genitalia
- Bounded by the pubic arch anteriorly and the transverse perineal ligament
- Contains the **superficial and deep perineal pouches**

### Anal Triangle (Posterior)
- Contains the **anal canal** and **ischioanal fossae**
- Bounded by the coccyx posteriorly and the sacrotuberous ligaments laterally

---

## Superficial Perineal Pouch

The superficial perineal pouch is the space between the **perineal membrane** (deep layer of the urogenital diaphragm) and the **Colles fascia** (superficial perineal fascia, a continuation of Scarpa's fascia).

### Contents in the Male
- **Root of the penis**: two crura and the bulb
  - **Corpus cavernosum** (pair): continuation of the crura
  - **Corpus spongiosum**: continuation of the bulb; surrounds the spongy urethra
- **Bulbospongiosus muscle**: surrounds the bulb of the penis
  - Contracts to empty the spongy urethra of residual semen and urine
  - Anterior fibres aid in maintaining erection
- **Ischiocavernosus muscle** (pair): surrounds each crus
  - Forces blood from cavernous spaces into corpora cavernosa to maintain erection
- **Superficial transverse perineal muscle**: from ischiopubic ramus to perineal body
- **Bulbourethral (Cowper's) glands**: pair of small glands; secrete pre-ejaculatory fluid
- **Internal pudendal artery branches**: superficial perineal arteries
- **Dorsal nerve of the penis** (branch of pudendal nerve)

### Contents in the Female
- **Vestibule of the vagina**
- **Clitoris** (erectile body; homologue of the penis)
  - **Crura of clitoris**: attach to ischiopubic rami
  - **Bulbs of the vestibule**: erectile tissue on either side of the vaginal orifice
- **Bulbospongiosus muscle**: surrounds the vaginal orifice
  - Constricts the vaginal orifice; aids in erection of clitoris
- **Ischiocavernosus muscle** (pair): surrounds each crus of clitoris
- **Superficial transverse perineal muscle**
- **Greater vestibular (Bartholin's) glands**: secrete mucus into the vestibule
- **Internal pudendal artery branches**: superficial perineal arteries
- **Perineal branch of pudendal nerve**

---

## Deep Perineal Pouch

The deep perineal pouch (deep perineal space) lies between the **perineal membrane** superiorly and the **inferior fascia of the urogenital diaphragm** (superior layer of the deep perineal pouch).

### Contents in the Male
- **Membranous urethra** (part of the male urethra passing through this space)
- **External urethral sphincter**: skeletal muscle surrounding the membranous urethra; voluntary control
- **Deep transverse perineal muscle**: runs between the ischiopubic rami; helps fix the perineal body
- **Bulbourethral (Cowper's) glands** (may extend into this space)
- **Internal pudendal artery**: branches to the deep structures
- **Dorsal nerve of the penis**

### Contents in the Female
- **Urethra** (passes through this space)
- **External urethral sphincter**: voluntary skeletal muscle surrounding the urethra
- **Vagina** (passes through this space)
- **Deep transverse perineal muscle**
- **Bulbourethral glands** (homologous to male Cowper's glands; smaller)
- **Internal pudendal artery branches**
- **Perineal nerve branches**

---

## Perineal Body

- A **fibromuscular midline structure** (central tendon of the perineum)
- Located between the anal canal posteriorly and the urogenital structures anteriorly
- **Point of attachment** for multiple muscles:
  - External anal sphincter
  - Bulbospongiosus
  - Superficial transverse perineal muscle
  - Deep transverse perineal muscle
  - Levator ani (pubococcygeus/puborectalis)
- **Clinical importance**: damage during childbirth can weaken the pelvic floor and contribute to pelvic organ prolapse or fecal incontinence

---

## Ischioanal Fossa

- Fat-filled space on each side of the anal canal and lower rectum
- **Boundaries**:
  - Medially: levator ani and external anal sphincter
  - Laterally: obturator internus and ischiopubic ramus
  - Posteriorly: sacrotuberous ligament
  - Anteriorly: perineal membrane
- **Contents**: adipose tissue, internal pudendal artery and vein, pudendal nerve, inferior rectal vessels and nerves
- **Clinical**: abscesses can form here (ischioanal/ischorectal abscess); can track from one side to the other via the **deep perineal space** (horseshoe abscess)

---

## Perineal Muscles

| Muscle | Origin | Insertion | Action | Nerve |
|--------|--------|-----------|--------|-------|
| **Bulbospongiosus** | Perineal body, central tendon of perineum | Median raphe (M), vestibular bulb (F) | Empties urethra of residual fluid; aids erection | Perineal nerve (S2-S4) |
| **Ischiocavernosus** | Ischiopubic ramus, ischial tuberosity | Crus of penis (M) / crus of clitoris (F) | Maintains erection by compressing crura | Perineal nerve (S2-S4) |
| **Superficial transverse perineal** | Ischiopubic ramus, ischial tuberosity | Perineal body | Stabilises the perineal body | Perineal nerve (S2-S4) |
| **Deep transverse perineal** | Ischiopubic ramus | Perineal body | Fixes/stabilises perineal body; supports urogenital diaphragm | Perineal nerve (S2-S4) |
| **External anal sphincter** | Anococcygeal raphe, coccyx, perineal body | Surrounds anal canal; blends with puborectalis | Voluntary control of defecation | Inferior rectal nerve (S2-S4) |

## Blood Supply of the Perineum

- **Internal pudendal artery** (branch of internal iliac artery): main arterial supply
  - Gives off: inferior rectal artery, perineal artery, artery to the bulb, deep artery of the penis/clitoris, dorsal artery of the penis/clitoris
- **Superficial and deep external pudendal arteries** (from femoral artery): contribute to scrotal/labial supply

## Venous Drainage

- **Internal pudendal vein** → internal iliac vein
- **Prostatic venous plexus** (male) receives blood from deep dorsal vein of penis
- In females: vesical and vaginal venous plexuses

## Nerve Supply

- **Pudendal nerve** (S2, S3, S4): the main nerve of the perineum
  - Gives off: inferior rectal nerve, perineal nerve, dorsal nerve of penis/clitoris
- **Perineal branch of posterior femoral cutaneous nerve** (S2, S3): supplies skin of posterior scrotum/labia

## Lymphatic Drainage

- Superficial inguinal lymph nodes (from skin and superficial structures)
- Internal iliac lymph nodes (from deeper structures)

---

## Colles Fascia (Superficial Perineal Fascia)

- The continuation of **Scarpa's fascia** from the anterior abdominal wall into the perineum
- In males: invests the penis as **superficial penile fascia** and the scrotum as **dartos fascia**
- In females: both Scarpa's and Camper's layers continue into the labia majora
- **Clinical**: fluid extravasation (e.g., from urethral rupture) can track from the perineum into the anterior abdominal wall along this fascia
