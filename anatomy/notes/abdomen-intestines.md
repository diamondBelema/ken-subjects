# Digestive System II — Small and Large Intestine

## Small Intestine

- Longest part of the GI tract (**5-7 m** long)
- Responsible for **90% of nutrient absorption** and most enzymatic digestion
- Extends from the **pyloric sphincter** to the **ileocecal valve**

### Main Functions

- **Digestion**: breaks down food using enzymes and bile
- **Absorption**: nutrients, electrolytes, vitamins, and water
- **Secretion**: mucus and enzymes
- **Immune defense**: gut-associated lymphoid tissue (GALT)

---

### Anatomical Subdivisions

| Part | Length | Key Features |
|---|---|---|
| **Duodenum** | ~25 cm | Shortest; receives chyme, bile, and pancreatic enzymes; Brunner's glands; **plicae circulares** |
| **Jejunum** | ~2.5 m | Major site of nutrient absorption; rich in plicae circulares and villi |
| **Ileum** | ~3.5 m | Longest; absorbs vitamin B12 and bile salts; **Peyer's patches** |

---

### Histological Layers (General)

#### 1. Mucosa

- **Epithelium**: simple columnar with goblet cells and absorptive **enterocytes** (microvilli form the **brush border**)
- **Villi**: finger-like projections of mucosa increasing surface area
  - Each villus contains **capillary network** (amino acids/sugars) and **lacteal** (fat absorption)
- **Crypts of Lieberkühn** (intestinal glands): invaginations between villi bases
  - Contain **Paneth cells** (antimicrobial), **stem cells**, and **enteroendocrine cells**
  - Secrete intestinal juice rich in enzymes and mucus
- **Lamina propria**: loose connective tissue with capillaries, lacteals, immune cells, and GALT
  - **Peyer's patches**: longitudinal aggregations of 30-50 lymphoid nodules in the ileum
- **Muscularis mucosae**: thin smooth muscle layer; moves villi independently to aid secretion and absorption

#### 2. Submucosa

- Dense irregular connective tissue
- Contains blood vessels, lymphatics, and **Meissner's plexus**
- **Duodenum only**: contains **Brunner's glands** (secrete alkaline mucus to neutralize gastric acid)

#### 3. Muscularis Externa

- Inner circular and outer longitudinal smooth muscle
- **Auerbach's (myenteric) plexus** between the two layers controls motility

#### 4. Serosa or Adventitia

- **Serosa** (visceral peritoneum): mesothelium + connective tissue in intraperitoneal portions
- **Adventitia**: connective tissue only in retroperitoneal parts of duodenum

---

### Cell Types in the Mucosa

| Cell Type | Location | Function |
|---|---|---|
| **Enterocytes** | Surface epithelium | Absorb nutrients; apical brush border with enzymes (disaccharidases, peptidases) |
| **Goblet cells** | Scattered among enterocytes | Secrete mucus; increase in number toward ileum |
| **Paneth cells** | Base of crypts | Contain eosinophilic granules with **lysozyme, defensins**; control gut microbiome |
| **Enteroendocrine cells** | Crypt base and villi (~1%) | Secrete **secretin, CCK, GIP** to regulate digestion and motility |
| **Stem (basal) cells** | Base of crypts | Proliferate rapidly; replace all epithelial types every **3-5 days** |
| **M cells** | Over Peyer's patches (ileum) | Antigen transport to underlying immune cells; immune surveillance |

---

### Histology of the Duodenum

**Identifying features:**
- **Brunner's glands** in submucosa (most distinctive)
- Broad or leaf-shaped villi
- Fewer goblet cells
- No Peyer's patches
- **Plicae circulares**: few and small

### Histology of the Jejunum

**Identifying features:**
- No Brunner's glands
- **Tall, finger-like villi**
- **Numerous and prominent plicae circulares**
- No Peyer's patches
- Moderate number of goblet cells
- Rich in capillaries

### Histology of the Ileum

**Identifying features:**
- No Brunner's glands
- **Shorter villi** and fewer plicae circulares
- **Peyer's patches** (lymphoid nodules for immune surveillance)
- **Highest density of goblet cells**
- Specialized for **vitamin B12 and bile salt absorption**

### Comparison Table

| Feature | Duodenum | Jejunum | Ileum |
|---|---|---|---|
| Villi | Broad/flat | Tall, finger-like | Short |
| Plicae circulares | Few, small | Numerous, prominent | Few, sparse |
| Brunner's glands | **Present** | Absent | Absent |
| Peyer's patches | Absent | Absent | **Present** |
| Goblet cells | Fewest | Moderate | **Most** |
| Glands | Brunner's | None submucosal | None submucosal |

## Large Intestine

- Forms the terminal part of the GI tract
- Begins at **ileocecal junction**, ends at the **anus**
- Approximately **1.5 m** long; shorter and less convoluted than the small intestine

### Components

1. **Cecum**: pouch-like; receives chyme from ileum via **ileocecal valve**; attached **vermiform appendix** with lymphoid tissue
2. **Colon**: ascending, transverse, descending, sigmoid
3. **Rectum**: straight, expandable; stores feces
4. **Anal canal**: terminal segment with internal and external sphincters

### Functional Roles

- **Absorption**: water, Na+, Cl-; forms semi-solid feces
- **Mucus secretion**: lubricates intestinal contents (goblet cells)
- **Fecal formation and storage**
- **Microbial activity**: gut microbiota ferment undigested carbohydrates, produce vitamin K and biotin, prevent pathogen colonization

---

### Gross Anatomical Features of the Colon

| Feature | Description |
|---|---|
| **Taeniae coli** | 3 bands of longitudinal muscle (outer layer is discontinuous) |
| **Haustra** | Sacculations formed by teniae coli shortening colon length |
| **Epiploic appendices** | Small fat-filled pouches on the serosal surface |

> In the **rectum**, longitudinal muscle becomes **continuous** (no teniae coli).

---

### Histological Layers of the Large Intestine

#### 1. Mucosa

- **Epithelium**: simple columnar with **colonocytes** (absorptive cells)
- **Lamina propria**: loose connective tissue rich in GALT (due to abundant bacteria)
- **Glands**: numerous straight tubular **crypts of Lieberkühn** (no villi, no plicae circulares)
- **Goblet cells**: increase in number distally, most abundant in rectum
- **Muscularis mucosae**: thin smooth muscle layer
- **No Paneth cells** in the large intestine

#### 2. Submucosa

- Dense irregular connective tissue with blood vessels, lymphatics, nerves, and **Meissner's plexus**

#### 3. Muscularis Externa

- Inner circular layer: continuous and uniform
- Outer longitudinal layer: **discontinuous**, aggregated into **3 teniae coli**
- **Auerbach's plexus** between the two layers

#### 4. Serosa or Adventitia

- **Serosa** in intraperitoneal regions (transverse and sigmoid colon)
- **Adventitia** in retroperitoneal segments (ascending and descending colon)

---

### Cell Types in Large Intestine Mucosa

| Cell Type | Function |
|---|---|
| **Goblet cells** | Secrete mucus for lubrication and protection; increase distally |
| **Colonocytes** | Absorb water and electrolytes; short apical microvilli |
| **Enteroendocrine cells** | Secrete peptide YY, serotonin (5-HT), somatostatin; regulate motility and appetite |
| **Stem cells** | At base of crypts; continuous renewal of epithelium |

---

## Ileocecal Valve

- Regulates passage of chyme from **ileum into cecum**
- Prevents backflow of colonic contents into the small intestine
- Competent valve helps prevent bacterial overgrowth of the small bowel

---

## Mesentery

- Double layer of **visceral peritoneum**
- Connects intraperitoneal organs to the posterior abdominal wall
- Provides pathway for **nerves, blood vessels, and lymphatics**
- **Mesentery of small intestine** = "the mesentery"
- Named according to the viscera it connects:
  - **Transverse mesocolon**
  - **Sigmoid mesocolon**
  - **Mesoappendix**

---

## Appendix

- Blind-ended tube attached to the **posteromedial wall of cecum**
- Length: **6-10 cm**; diameter: ~6 mm
- Rich in **GALT** (lymphoid follicles in lamina propria and submucosa)
- In early life: reservoir for beneficial bacteria
- **Appendicitis**: obstruction by fecoliths or lymphoid hyperplasia → inflammation → possible perforation

### Histological Layers of the Appendix

1. **Mucosa**: simple columnar epithelium with abundant goblet cells; crypts of Lieberkühn; muscularis mucosae may be incomplete or absent
2. **Submucosa**: prominent lymphoid nodules, blood vessels, nerves
3. **Muscularis externa**: inner circular and outer longitudinal smooth muscle; **no teniae coli**
4. **Serosa**: outer mesothelium covering
