# Respiratory Gas Exchange (RESP III)

## Physical Principles of Gaseous Exchange

1. Gases are in random motion and diffuse from **higher concentration to lower concentration**
2. Kinetic energy is provided by the motion of the molecules
3. Total pressure of a gas is directly proportional to the concentration of gas molecules
4. Pressure of gas in solution is also determined by its **solubility coefficient**

### Solubility Coefficients

| Gas | Solubility Coefficient |
|---|---|
| O₂ | 0.024 |
| CO₂ | 0.57 |
| CO | 0.18 |
| N₂ | 0.012 |
| He | 0.008 |

- CO₂ has a **high** solubility coefficient and is attracted to water
- Other gases have **low** solubility coefficients and are repelled by water

### Henry's Law

$$\text{Pressure} = \frac{\text{Concentration of dissolved gas}}{\text{Solubility coefficient}}$$

---

## Dalton's Law

The total pressure of a gas mixture equals the sum of the partial pressures of each individual gas:

$$P_{\text{total}} = P_1 + P_2 + P_3 + \ldots$$

## Gas Exchange at the Alveoli

- Gas exchange occurs across the **alveolar-capillary membrane** (respiratory membrane)
- Diffusion driven by **partial pressure gradients**:
  - O₂ diffuses from alveolus (PO₂ 100) → capillary blood (PO₂ 40)
  - CO₂ diffuses from capillary blood (PCO₂ 46) → alveolus (PCO₂ 40)
- Despite the smaller PCO₂ gradient, CO₂ exchange is efficient due to its **high solubility** (20× that of O₂)

### Why Arterial PO₂ < Alveolar PO₂

- **Thebesian veins**: drain blood from myocardium directly into left ventricle
- **Bronchopulmonary anastomoses**: bronchial venous blood (systemic) drains into pulmonary veins
- These create a small **physiological shunt**

---

## Gas Exchange at the Tissues

- O₂ diffuses from arterial blood (PO₂ ~95–100) → tissue cells (PO₂ ~40)
- CO₂ diffuses from tissue cells (PCO₂ ~46) → capillary blood (PCO₂ ~40)
- Driven by the same partial pressure gradient principle

---

## Factors Affecting Gas Exchange

| Factor | Effect on Gas Exchange |
|---|---|
| **Pressure difference** | Larger gradient → faster diffusion |
| **Thickness of respiratory membrane** | Normally ~0.5 μm; increased thickness (fibrosis) reduces diffusion |
| **Surface area** | Reduced in emphysema/pneumonia → impedes exchange |
| **Solubility of gas** | Higher solubility → faster diffusion (CO₂ >> O₂) |

---

## Diffusing Capacity

Volume of gas that diffuses through the membrane each minute for a pressure difference of **1 mmHg**.

| Gas | Diffusing Capacity |
|---|---|
| **O₂** | ~21 ml/min/mmHg |
| **CO₂** | 400–450 ml/min/mmHg (20× greater than O₂) |

- Diffusing capacity is **directly proportional** to surface area of respiratory membrane
- Diffusing capacity is **inversely proportional** to thickness of respiratory membrane
- **Exercise** increases diffusing capacity of O₂, CO₂, and CO

---

## Ventilation Effects on Partial Pressures

- Adequate ventilation ensures alveolar PO₂ remains high and PCO₂ remains low
- **Hypoventilation**: ↓alveolar PO₂, ↑alveolar PCO₂ → impaired gas exchange
- **Hyperventilation**: ↑alveolar PO₂, ↓alveolar PCO₂ → increased driving force for O₂ diffusion
- Regional ventilation differences exist: more ventilation in lower lung zones due to gravity
