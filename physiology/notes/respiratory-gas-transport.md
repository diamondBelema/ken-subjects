# Respiratory Gas Transport (RESP IV)

## O₂ Transport

O₂ delivery to tissues depends on:
- Amount of O₂ entering lungs
- Adequacy of pulmonary gas exchange
- Blood flow to tissue
- Degree of constriction of vascular bed in tissue
- Cardiac output
- Capacity of blood to carry O₂

### Forms of O₂ Transport

| Form | Amount in Arterial Blood | % of Total |
|---|---|---|
| **Dissolved in plasma** | 0.29 ml/100 ml | ~1.5% |
| **Bound to hemoglobin** | 19.5 ml/100 ml | ~98.5% |
| **Total arterial O₂ content** | 19.8 ml/100 ml | 100% |

### Hemoglobin-Oxygen Binding

- 1 g Hb binds **1.34 ml O₂** at 100% saturation (theoretical capacity: 1.39 ml, reduced by methemoglobin and CO)
- 100 ml blood with 15 g Hb fully saturated = **20.1 ml O₂**
- At **97% systemic saturation**: 1.30 ml O₂/g Hb → 19.8 ml/100 ml blood

### Venous Blood

- Hb is **75% saturated** at rest
- Total O₂ content: **15.3 ml/100 ml**
  - Dissolved: 0.1 ml (0.7%)
  - Hb-bound: 15.2 ml (99.3%)

### O₂ Extraction at Rest

- **4.5 ml O₂/100 ml** extracted from blood (19.8 − 15.3)
  - 0.17 ml (3.8%) from dissolved form
  - 4.33 ml (96.2%) from Hb-bound form

## Hemoglobin

- Protein with **4 subunits** (quaternary structure)
- Each subunit: heme moiety + polypeptide chain
- Heme: porphyrin ring + **1 ferrous iron (Fe²⁺) atom**
- Each of 4 Fe²⁺ atoms binds **1 O₂ reversibly** → reaction is **oxygenation** (iron stays ferrous)
- Quaternary structure determines O₂ affinity

### Oxygenation Reaction

$$Hb_4 + O_2 \rightleftharpoons Hb_4O_2$$
$$Hb_4O_2 + O_2 \rightleftharpoons Hb_4O_4$$
$$Hb_4O_4 + O_2 \rightleftharpoons Hb_4O_6$$
$$Hb_4O_6 + O_2 \rightleftharpoons Hb_4O_8$$

### Oxygen-Hemoglobin Dissociation Curve

- **Sigmoid-shaped** curve
- Reflects cooperative binding: binding of first O₂ molecule increases affinity for subsequent molecules

### P₅₀

- PO₂ at which Hb is **half-saturated** (50%) with O₂
- **Normal P₅₀ ≈ 27 mmHg**
- Higher P₅₀ = lower O₂ affinity = right shift
- Lower P₅₀ = higher O₂ affinity = left shift

---

## Factors Affecting Hb-O₂ Affinity (Dissociation Curve)

### Right Shift (↓Affinity → ↑O₂ Unloading)

| Factor | Direction |
|---|---|
| **Decreased pH** | ↑ |
| **Increased PCO₂** | ↑ |
| **Increased temperature** | ↑ |
| **Increased 2,3-DPG** | ↑ |

- Occurs in **active tissues and exercise**: high PCO₂, low pH, high temperature → more O₂ delivered where needed

### Left Shift (↑Affinity → ↓O₂ Unloading)

| Factor | Direction |
|---|---|
| **Increased pH** | ↓ |
| **Decreased PCO₂** | ↓ |
| **Decreased temperature** | ↓ |
| **Decreased 2,3-DPG** | ↓ |

### Bohr Effect

- **Decrease in O₂ affinity of Hb when blood pH falls**
- Related to the fact that deoxygenated Hb binds H⁺ more actively than oxyhemoglobin
- In tissues: ↑CO₂ → ↑H⁺ → reduced O₂ affinity → O₂ unloading enhanced
- In lungs: ↑O₂ → ↑H⁺ released → ↑CO₂ excretion

### 2,3-DPG (2,3-Diphosphoglycerate)

Factors affecting 2,3-DPG concentration:
- **pH**: 2,3-DPG falls when pH is low
- **Exercise**: increases 2,3-DPG
- **High altitude**: increases 2,3-DPG (acclimatization)
- **Hormones**: thyroid hormones, growth hormone, androgens increase 2,3-DPG

## Myoglobin vs Hemoglobin

| Feature | Hemoglobin | Myoglobin |
|---|---|---|
| **Structure** | 4 subunits (tetramer) | Single polypeptide chain (monomer) |
| **O₂ binding** | 4 mol O₂ per mole Hb | 1 mol O₂ per mole |
| **Dissociation curve** | Sigmoid | Rectangular hyperbola |
| **P₅₀** | ~27 mmHg | ~2.8 mmHg |
| **Location** | Red blood cells | Skeletal muscle |
| **Function** | O₂ transport | O₂ storage; picks up O₂ from Hb; releases O₂ only at very low PO₂ (e.g. exercising muscle); facilitates diffusion of O₂ from blood to mitochondria |

## CO₂ Transport

- Venous blood contains **~52.7 ml CO₂ per 100 ml**
- ~200 ml CO₂ produced per minute in the body

### Three Forms of CO₂ Transport

| Form | % of Total | Location | Details |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Dissolved** | 10% | Plasma | Proportional to PCO₂ |
| **Carbamino compounds** | 30% | On Hb (and other proteins) | CO₂ binds to amino groups of Hb reversibly |
| **Bicarbonate (HCO₃⁻)** | 60% | Plasma (formed in RBCs) | Main form of transport |

### Carbamino Compound Formation

$$R\text{-}NH_2 + CO_2 \rightleftharpoons R\text{-}COO^- + H^+$$

- CO₂ binds directly to Hb (no enzyme required)
- Favored by **deoxygenated Hb** (deoxyhemoglobin binds CO₂ more readily)

### Bicarbonate Formation

$$CO_2 + H_2O \xrightarrow{\text{carbonic anhydrase}} H_2CO_3 \rightleftharpoons H^+ + HCO_3^-$$

- Reaction catalyzed by **carbonic anhydrase** inside red blood cells (absent in plasma)
- Carbonic acid dissociates into H⁺ and HCO₃⁻
- ~70% of HCO₃⁻ leaves the RBC in exchange for Cl⁻

---

## Chloride Shift

- The exchange of **HCO₃⁻ for Cl⁻** across the RBC membrane
- Mediated by the membrane protein **Band 3 (AE1)**
- Occurs in systemic capillaries as CO₂ enters RBCs:
  - HCO₃⁻ accumulates in RBC → diffuses out into plasma
  - Cl⁻ moves into RBC to maintain electrical neutrality
- **Reverse chloride shift** occurs in pulmonary capillaries:
  - HCO₃⁻ re-enters RBC → converted back to CO₂ → diffuses into alveoli

---

## Haldane Effect

- **Deoxygenated Hb carries more CO₂ than oxygenated Hb**
- In tissues: O₂ dissociation from Hb → ↑CO₂ carrying capacity → more CO₂ picked up
- In alveoli: O₂ binding to Hb → ↓CO₂ carrying capacity → CO₂ released for exhalation
- High PO₂ in alveoli causes O₂ to replace CO₂ on Hb

---

## CO-O₂ Interaction

- **CO binds to Hb ~210× more strongly than O₂**
- Forms **carboxyhemoglobin (HbCO)**
- CO displaces O₂ from Hb → **CO poisoning**
- Sources: smoke inhalation, suicide attempts, smog, cigarette smoking
- Carboxyhemoglobin in active nonsmokers: ≤1.5%
- **Treatment**: hyperbaric oxygen (100% O₂ at 2–3 atmospheres)
