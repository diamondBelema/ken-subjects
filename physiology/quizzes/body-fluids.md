  options:
  - 1/4 (about 3 L)
  - 1/2 (about 7 L)
  - 3/4 (about 11 L)

  - Blood volume = Plasma volume / (1 - Hematocrit)

- id: phy-q014
  type: mcq
  question: Which of the following is a cause of water intoxication?
  options:
  - Diarrhea
  - Excessive sweating
  - SIADH (syndrome of inappropriate ADH secretion)
  - Diabetes insipidus
  answer: 3
  explanation: SIADH causes hypersecretion of ADH, leading to excessive water retention
    and water intoxication. Other causes include heart failure and renal disorders.

- id: phy-q015
  type: mcq
  question: What is the primary treatment for severe water intoxication?
  options:
  - Oral rehydration salts
  - ADH receptor antagonists and diuretics
  - Blood transfusion
  - Antibiotics
  answer: 2
  explanation: Severe water intoxication is treated with diuretics (increase water
    loss), ADH receptor antagonists (prevent water reabsorption), and IV saline to
    restore Na+.
