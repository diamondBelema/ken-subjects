## Notes: na-002
Remember: B = biological (predominant in vivo, ~20 Å), A = found with RNA (~26 Å, widest), Z = zigzag (left-handed, ~18 Å, narrowest).

## Notes: na-026
Key point: The 2'-OH in RNA is the reason RNA is less stable than DNA in basic conditions. This is why DNA (lacking 2'-OH) is the preferred genetic material.

## Notes: na-036
Mnemonic for the synthesis cycle: "DCOC" — Detritylation, Coupling, Oxidation, Capping. The first 3 are theoretically sufficient; capping is industrial.

## Notes: na-040
Methylation pattern: Cytosine + SAM → 5-methylcytosine + SAH. Catalyzed by DNMTs. Occurs at CpG islands near promoters.

## Notes: na-046
Taq polymerase leaves 5'-OH on PCR products (unless phosphorylated primers were used). Ligation requires a 5' phosphate. T4 PNK provides this.

## Notes: na-017b
Supercoiling arises naturally during replication/transcription. Topoisomerases regulate it. Intercalating drugs can alter it but aren't the primary cause.
